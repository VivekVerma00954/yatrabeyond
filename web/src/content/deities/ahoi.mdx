quickIdentity: >
  Ahoi Mata is a North Indian folk goddess invoked by mothers for the long
  life and well-being of their children, worshipped above all through the
  annual Ahoi Ashtami fast observed a little over a week before Diwali.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Called **Ahoi Bhagwati** in devotional speech, she is most commonly held
  to be a benevolent form of Parvati and protector of offspring, though some
  regional accounts identify her instead with Lakshmi.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She has no single classical temple image; her presence on the vrat is
  marked instead through a hand-drawn or painted image (ahoi mata ke chitra),
  often eight-cornered, and through a silver ahoi pendant, engraved with
  children and a moon, that observant mothers wear.

keyStories: >
  In the best-known telling, a woman digging soil in a forest to prepare her
  home for Diwali accidentally kills a young animal, in most versions a
  hoglet, in some a lion cub. Her seven sons subsequently come to harm, and
  it is only after she recognises her fault, repents, and takes up devoted
  fasting and worship of Ahoi Mata that the goddess relents and restores them
  to her.

familyAndRelations: >
  She has little independent genealogy in classical mythology; her identity
  is defined almost entirely by her role as guardian of children (santaan)
  rather than by a fixed family lineage of her own.

significance: >
  Ahoi Ashtami falls on Kartik Krishna Ashtami, in the Purnimant calendar
  followed across North India. Mothers keep a strict nirjala (waterless) fast
  from sunrise until stars or moonrise, breaking it only after the ahoi katha
  is told and the goddess worshipped for their children's protection.

regionalVariation: >
  Observance is strongest in Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana and Rajasthan.
  The precise animal in the founding legend, and details of its telling, vary
  from household to household and region to region, a reminder that the
  katha survives mainly through oral, family transmission rather than a
  single fixed text.
