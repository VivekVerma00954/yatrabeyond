quickIdentity: >
  Lalita Tripurasundari, "the beautiful one who plays across the three
  worlds," is the supreme goddess of the Srividya tradition within
  Shaktism, and is counted among the ten Mahavidyas, the great tantric
  wisdom-goddesses.

namesAndEpithets: >
  She is also called **Shodashi**, **Kamakshi**, and **Rajarajeshvari**,
  "empress of empresses." Her epithet Lalita, "she who plays," reflects the
  Srividya teaching that all creation, preservation and dissolution is
  itself the Goddess's play (lila).

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She is depicted as radiantly beautiful, typically red-hued, seated on a
  lotus or on a throne resting on Shiva's reclining form, with four arms
  bearing a noose, a goad, a sugarcane bow and five flower arrows, emblems
  read as tools of attraction and control over the mind rather than
  weapons of war.

keyStories: >
  Within the Srikula branch of Shaktism she is honoured as the foremost of
  the Mahavidyas and the primary goddess of Srividya, worshipped in three
  linked forms: a radiant embodied (sthula) form, a subtle mantra form as
  the Srividya mantra, and a supreme geometric form as the Sri Chakra or
  Sri Yantra, through which her presence is meditated upon directly.

familyAndRelations: >
  Regarded in Srikula theology as the supreme Devi herself, from whom other
  goddesses and gods, including Shiva in his form beneath her throne, are
  understood to derive; she is not treated as a subordinate consort within
  this tradition.

significance: >
  Her worship, centred on the Sri Chakra and the thousand-name hymn Lalita
  Sahasranama, is one of the most intellectually developed and initiatory
  branches of Shakta practice, historically transmitted through a guru
  lineage rather than open public temple ritual.

regionalVariation: >
  Srividya worship of her is practised across India but has particularly
  strong lineages in South India, where she is also identified with
  Kamakshi of Kanchipuram; practice and emphasis vary between different
  Srividya sampradayas.
