quickIdentity: >
  Swaminarayan (1781-1830), born Ghanshyam and widely known by his
  monastic name Sahajanand Swami, is venerated by his followers as a
  manifestation of Krishna and as the founder of the Swaminarayan
  Sampradaya, a devotional tradition centred in Gujarat and now established
  worldwide.

namesAndEpithets: >
  As a child ascetic he travelled under the name **Neelkanth**; on
  initiation his guru named him **Sahajanand Swami**; the name
  **Swaminarayan** comes from the mantra he later taught his followers, and
  the sampraday itself takes its name from him.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is depicted as a serene ascetic, typically in saffron or yellow robes.
  Temple worship across the tradition's branches centres on murtis of
  Swaminarayan himself, often installed alongside Radha-Krishna or
  Lakshmi-Narayan, the specific pairing varying by branch and mandir.

keyStories: >
  Orphaned as a child, he left home at the age of eleven and spent seven
  years wandering across India as a young yogi under the name Neelkanth
  before settling at the hermitage of the ascetic Ramanand Swami. Initiated
  in 1800 and given leadership of Ramanand's Uddhava Sampradaya in 1802
  despite some opposition, he went on to build six major mandirs, at
  Ahmedabad, Bhuj, Vadtal, Dholera, Junagadh and Gadhada, and to lead
  large-scale nonviolent yajnas alongside social reforms for women and the
  poor.

familyAndRelations: >
  He succeeded his own guru, Ramanand Swami, as head of what became the
  Swaminarayan Sampradaya. Before his death he established two acharya
  lineages, at Ahmedabad (Nar Narayan Dev Gadi) and Vadtal (Laxmi Narayan
  Dev Gadi), from which the tradition's later branches, including BAPS,
  descend.

significance: >
  His teachings on personal morality, nonviolence and devotion are recorded
  in texts including the Shikshapatri and the Vachanamrut, and remain
  foundational across the sampraday's branches. The tradition he founded has
  grown into one of the most visible modern Gujarati Hindu movements, with a
  significant global diaspora presence.

regionalVariation: >
  Rooted in Gujarat, the tradition today spans a large global Gujarati
  diaspora. Its branches, among them BAPS and the Nar Narayan Dev and Laxmi
  Narayan Dev gadis, differ in succession and organisational structure while
  all tracing their authority back to Swaminarayan himself.
