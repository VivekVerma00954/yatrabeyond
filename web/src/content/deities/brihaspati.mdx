quickIdentity: >
  Brihaspati is the Devguru, the divine preceptor of the gods, and, as one
  of the Navagraha, personifies the planet Jupiter and rules Thursday
  (Brihaspativara). He is counted among the most consistently benevolent of
  the nine grahas.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is called simply **Guru**, "teacher," a title so closely tied to him
  that it names the planet Jupiter itself in everyday astrological speech;
  Thursday, Brihaspativara or Guruvar, likewise takes its name from him.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is shown as a large, golden-complexioned figure, richly adorned, often
  seated on a lotus or riding a chariot, carrying a staff of learning and a
  vessel or rosary that mark him as a preceptor rather than a warrior deity,
  in keeping with his role as the counsellor of the gods.

keyStories: >
  Son of the sage Angiras, one of the Saptarishi, he appears already in the
  Rigveda as a sage born from the first great light, bright and pure,
  carrying a bow strung with Rta, cosmic order. As Devguru he serves as
  counsellor to Indra during the long war between the gods and the asuras,
  his wisdom repeatedly tipping contests of strength toward the gods'
  favour.

familyAndRelations: >
  Son of the sage Angiras; some traditions also describe him as a
  mind-born son of Brahma. As Devguru he stands at the head of the gods'
  own line of teachers, distinct from Shukra, who fills the parallel role of
  guru to the asuras.

significance: >
  As the Navagraha most associated with wisdom, knowledge, children and
  wealth, he is considered the most consistently auspicious of the nine
  grahas in Vedic astrology, and is propitiated especially on Thursdays for
  guidance, learning and prosperity.

regionalVariation: >
  He is worshipped chiefly through the Navagraha shrine set found in temple
  courtyards across India, alongside a smaller number of dedicated temples,
  such as the modern Brihaspati Dham in Jaipur, that focus specifically on
  his worship.
