quickIdentity: >
  Vithoba, also called Vitthala or Panduranga, is a form of Krishna, and so
  of Vishnu, worshipped chiefly in Maharashtra and Karnataka as the
  presiding deity of Pandharpur and the focal point of the Varkari
  devotional tradition.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is called **Panduranga**, and, in the loving, familiar register of
  Marathi devotional poetry, simply **Vitthal**. His consort, Rukmini, is
  addressed as **Rakhumai**.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is depicted as a dark-skinned young boy, standing with his hands set
  akimbo on his hips, upright on a brick, an unusually informal, unarmed
  posture for a major Vaishnava deity that Varkari poet-saints read as a
  sign of his approachability and love for his devotees.

keyStories: >
  Legend holds that his devotee Pundalik, absorbed in caring for his aged
  parents, asked the visiting god to wait rather than interrupt his duty
  and threw down a brick for him to stand on; Vithoba is said to have
  stood on that very brick at Pandharpur ever since, waiting for his
  devotees. Generations of Varkari poet-saints, among them Tukaram,
  Namdev, Eknath and Dnyaneshwar, composed devotional songs (abhangs) to
  him that remain central to Marathi devotional literature.

familyAndRelations: >
  Understood as a form of Krishna, and through him of Vishnu; his consort
  Rukmini is worshipped alongside him at Pandharpur as Rakhumai.

significance: >
  He is the object of the Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi pilgrimages (wari) to
  Pandharpur, in which hundreds of thousands of Varkari pilgrims walk for
  days singing abhangs, one of the largest and oldest continuous walking
  pilgrimages in the world.

regionalVariation: >
  His cult is centred on Maharashtra, with a significant following in
  Karnataka as well; the Varkari tradition's emphasis on devotional song
  over ritual formality, and its openness across caste lines, distinguishes
  it within wider Vaishnavism.
