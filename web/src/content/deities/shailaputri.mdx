quickIdentity: >
  Shailaputri, "daughter of the mountain," is the first of the Navadurga, the
  nine forms of Durga worshipped across the nine nights of Navratri. She is
  Parvati, daughter of the Himalayan mountain-king Himavat, and marks the
  goddess's fresh beginning after her previous life as Sati.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Her name joins *shaila* (mountain) and *putri* (daughter). She is also
  called **Sati Bhavani**, **Parvati** and **Himavati**, and is described as
  embodying the combined power of Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She is shown with two arms and a crescent moon on her forehead, holding a
  trident in her right hand and a lotus in her left, riding the bull Nandi.
  In yogic tradition she is associated with the root chakra, Muladhara, from
  which the awakened energy begins its ascent toward Shiva at the crown.

keyStories: >
  In her previous life she was Sati, daughter of Daksha. When Daksha held a
  great yagna and pointedly did not invite her husband Shiva, Sati attended
  anyway; Daksha insulted Shiva before the assembly, and Sati, unable to bear
  it, immolated herself in the sacrificial fire. She was reborn as Parvati,
  daughter of Himavat, and in time married Shiva again. Navratri's opening
  Ghatasthapana ritual, establishing a sacred pot as the seat of Shakti for
  the nine days, is performed on her day.

familyAndRelations: >
  Daughter of Himavat and Menavati; consort Shiva. She is best understood not
  as a separate figure but as the first stage of Parvati's own unfolding
  across the nine Navadurga forms.

significance: >
  Her day opens Navratri and is considered auspicious for beginning yogic
  practice and taking initiations, marking the goddess's fresh start after
  the Sati episode.

regionalVariation: >
  Named shrines include a Shailaputri temple at Marhia Ghat in Varanasi, one
  at Baramulla in Jammu and Kashmir, a cave shrine at Zewan in Srinagar where
  she is worshipped as a shila (rock), and a temple in Sianj village, Mandi
  district, Himachal Pradesh, where she is honoured as a Rajmata of the
  Mandi region.
