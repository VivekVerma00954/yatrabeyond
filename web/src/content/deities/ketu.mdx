quickIdentity: >
  Ketu is one of the Navagraha, personifying the descending lunar node,
  astronomically the point where the Moon's orbit crosses the ecliptic
  moving southward, and is held responsible in myth for lunar eclipses.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Paired inseparably with Rahu, he is understood as the other half of the
  demon **Svarbhanu**, specifically his headless body, in contrast to Rahu,
  the severed head.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is typically depicted as a torso without a head, or with a body
  ending abruptly, sometimes shown with a serpent's tail, an image directly
  reflecting his origin as the decapitated remainder of Svarbhanu after
  Vishnu's beheading.

keyStories: >
  During the Samudra Manthana, Vishnu beheaded the asura Svarbhanu after he
  disguised himself as a deva to steal a share of the amrita; the severed
  head became Rahu, and the headless, still-immortal body became Ketu. As
  the two halves of one being, always opposite each other in the sky, they
  jointly cause eclipses, Rahu of the sun, Ketu classically associated with
  the moon.

familyAndRelations: >
  Rahu and Ketu together form the two halves of the single asura
  Svarbhanu, an orbital pair fixed permanently 180 degrees apart from one
  another in both the sky and astrological charts.

significance: >
  As a headless "shadow planet," Ketu is associated in Vedic astrology with
  detachment, spirituality, karmic residue from past actions, and sudden
  loss balanced against spiritual insight, considered both malefic and,
  through the very sorrow it brings, ultimately redirecting toward
  spiritual growth.

regionalVariation: >
  Like Rahu, he is worshipped almost exclusively within the standard
  Navagraha temple set rather than through independent shrines, with a
  small number of dedicated Ketu temples forming part of regional Navagraha
  pilgrimage circuits, particularly in Tamil Nadu.
