quickIdentity: >
  Jhulelal is the most revered deity of Sindhi Hindus, worshipped as an
  avatar or manifestation of the water god Varuna (in some accounts of
  Vishnu), whose birth is celebrated each year at Cheti Chand, the Sindhi
  new year.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is also known as **Uderolal**, and is honoured by Sindhi Muslims as
  well under the shared name **Zinda Pir**, "the living saint," reflecting
  a following that has historically crossed religious lines along the
  Indus.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is typically depicted as a serene, crowned figure seated on a fish or
  a lotus, often shown as a child on a swing (giving him the name
  "Jhulelal," "the swinging one"), holding a water vessel that marks his
  identity with Varuna and the river.

keyStories: >
  Tradition holds that when a tyrannical ruler, remembered as Mirkshah,
  ordered the Hindus of Sindh to convert to Islam or face death, they
  prayed to the river Indus, and Varuna promised to take human birth to
  save them. Born as a child of extraordinary powers, he grew to preach
  that Hindus and Muslims worshipped the same God, ultimately persuading
  the ruler to relent, a story that positions him as a unifying,
  cross-community figure from the start.

familyAndRelations: >
  Regarded principally as an avatar of Varuna, the Vedic god of water and
  the cosmic order, though some traditions instead describe him as an
  avatar of Vishnu.

significance: >
  His birth is celebrated at Cheti Chand, in the month of Chaitra, marking
  both the Sindhi new year and a thanksgiving to Varuna, observed with
  processions and river worship by Sindhi Hindu communities in India and
  across the diaspora.

regionalVariation: >
  His cult is specifically tied to Sindhi identity, historically centred on
  the Indus river region and today sustained by Sindhi communities
  relocated to India and abroad after Partition.
