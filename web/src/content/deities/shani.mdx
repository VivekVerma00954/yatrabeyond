quickIdentity: >
  Shani is the divine personification of the planet Saturn, one of the
  Navagraha and ruler of Saturday. Unlike most other grahas he is also
  independently worshipped as a full deity of karma, time, discipline and
  justly delivered consequence.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is called **Shani Dev**, and, reflecting his reputation as a strict but
  fair judge, is described as the deity who "delivers results depending
  upon one's thoughts, speech and deeds," rather than acting arbitrarily.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is depicted as a dark-complexioned figure, often carrying a sword or a
  danda (sceptre), seated on a buffalo or, in some images, a crow. At his
  most widely visited shrine, Shani Shingnapur, he is represented not by a
  carved image but by a five-and-a-half-foot black rock installed on an
  open platform, alongside a trishula and a Nandi image.

keyStories: >
  He is the son of Surya, the sun, and Chhaya, the shadow-form of Surya's
  wife Sanjna, born of the tension within his father's household. His
  reputation for exacting, if ultimately fair, justice underlies the wide
  respect and even fear with which devotees approach him, seeking to placate
  rather than merely petition him.

familyAndRelations: >
  Son of Surya and Chhaya, and consequently a half-brother, in the wider
  solar family, to figures such as Yama associated with Surya's other
  consort Sanjna.

significance: >
  He governs longevity, hardship, discipline, delay, ambition and the
  wisdom gained through experience, and Saturday worship of him is widely
  practised to ease life's difficulties, especially through charity offered
  without expectation of return.

regionalVariation: >
  His best-known independent shrine is at Shani Shingnapur in Ahmednagar
  district, Maharashtra, a village famously built without doors on its
  houses in the belief that Shani himself punishes theft; elsewhere he is
  worshipped chiefly as part of the standard Navagraha temple set.
