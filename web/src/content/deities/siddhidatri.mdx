quickIdentity: >
  Siddhidatri, "giver of perfection," is the ninth and final of the
  Navadurga, goddess of siddhi (spiritual attainment), representing the
  goddess's own arrival at her highest, supreme form as Mahashakti.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Her name joins *siddhi* (spiritual attainment, perfection) and *datri*
  (giver, bestower). Her name is also commonly rendered **Siddhidhatri**.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She is shown with four arms holding a chakra (discus), a shankha (conch
  shell), a mace, and a lotus, seated on a fully bloomed lotus or, in some
  depictions, a lion. Some images show her flanked by gandharvas, yakshas,
  siddhas, asuras and devas, all paying her homage.

keyStories: >
  According to the legend recorded of her, at the dawn of creation Shiva
  worshipped the unmanifest form of the great goddess Mahadevi in order to
  perform the act of creation. The goddess is said to have emerged from the
  left half of Shiva's own body as Siddhidhatri, and to have then conferred
  upon him the ashta siddhi, the eight classical spiritual attainments.
  Because of this origin from Shiva's left side, she is closely associated
  with the composite Ardhanarishvara form, in which the left half represents
  Parvati.

familyAndRelations: >
  Consort Shiva, in the particular sense, in this legend, of having emerged
  from him rather than simply having married him.

significance: >
  Worshipped on Navami, Navratri's ninth and final night, marking the
  completion of the goddess's nine-stage unfolding into her supreme
  Mahashakti form. Separately, the Agni Purana lists an alternative set of
  nine names it also calls "Navadurgas" (Rudrachanda, Prachanda, Chandogra,
  Chandanayika, Chanda, Chandavati, Chandarupa, Atichandika, Ugrachanda),
  a distinct textual tradition worth noting rather than a contradiction of
  the nine forms described here.

regionalVariation: >
  No single named pilgrimage site stands out for her in the sources
  consulted; her worship follows the general Navami-night pattern that
  closes Navratri across regions.
