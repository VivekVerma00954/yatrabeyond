quickIdentity: >
  Savitri is the heroine of a celebrated Mahabharata episode (the
  Savitri-Satyavan Upakhyana, in the Vana Parva), honoured as the ideal of
  a devoted wife whose intelligence and steadfastness overturn a divine
  decree of her husband's death.

namesAndEpithets: >
  She is remembered chiefly through the epithet **Pativrata**, the
  devoted wife whose fidelity carries near-supernatural power, a quality
  the episode itself is sometimes titled to honour, Pativrata-mahatmya
  Parva.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She is not given a fixed temple icon; her presence is invoked instead
  through ritual action, most visibly the thread married women tie around
  a banyan (vat) tree during the Vat Purnima/Vat Savitri observance, the
  tree itself standing in for the story's forest setting.

keyStories: >
  Princess Savitri, daughter of King Ashvapati, chose as her husband
  Satyavan, an exiled prince living in the forest with his blind father
  Dyumatsena, despite being warned he was fated to die within a year. When
  Satyavan died as foretold, Yama, god of death, came to take his soul;
  Savitri followed them into the forest, and her arguments and unwavering
  devotion so moved Yama that he granted her a series of boons, ultimately
  restoring Satyavan's life.

familyAndRelations: >
  Daughter of King Ashvapati; wife of Satyavan, prince of a kingdom lost by
  his blind father Dyumatsena, whose own sight and kingdom Savitri's boons
  from Yama also restore.

significance: >
  Her story is retold each year at Vat Purnima (Vat Savitri), observed by
  married women across North India and parts of Maharashtra, Goa and
  Gujarat, who fast and tie thread around a banyan tree in prayer for
  their husbands' long life, directly modelled on Savitri's own devotion.

regionalVariation: >
  The observance and the precise timing of Vat Purnima/Vat Savitri differ
  by region, some communities marking it on the full moon (Purnima) of
  Jyeshtha and others on the preceding new moon, though the underlying
  story and its meaning remain consistent.
