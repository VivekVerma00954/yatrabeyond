quickIdentity: >
  Dattatreya, or Datta, is worshipped as the combined avatar of the
  Trimurti, Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva together in a single being, and as the
  archetype of the wandering renunciate guru, lord of yoga and teacher
  without formal discourse.

namesAndEpithets: >
  His name joins **Datta** ("given") and **Atreya** ("of Atri," his sage
  father). He is also called **Dattaguru**, and in ascetic depictions
  **Digambara**, "sky-clad," reflecting the unconventional, boundary-crossing
  Avadhuta tradition of teachers associated with him.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is classically shown with three heads, of Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva, and
  six arms bearing their respective emblems, water-pot and rosary for
  Brahma, conch and mace for Vishnu, trident and drum for Shiva. He is
  typically accompanied by four dogs, said to represent the four Vedas, and a
  cow, representing sanctity and Brahmanical tradition.

keyStories: >
  Puranic accounts hold that the sage Atri's wife Anasuya was so devoted and
  chaste that the Trimurti, testing her, was compelled instead to grant her
  the boon of having all three born to her and Atri as sons, of whom
  Dattatreya, embodying all three together, is the most celebrated. As a
  wandering guru he is said to have accepted disciples across boundaries of
  caste and status, teaching, in the Avadhuta tradition linked to him, more
  through example than formal instruction.

familyAndRelations: >
  Son of the sage Atri and his wife Anasuya. Various traditions name him
  guru to figures including Parashurama, Sahasrarjuna and Kartavirya, and
  over time he has increasingly been worshipped as an independent deity
  rather than strictly as an avatar of the Trimurti.

significance: >
  He is central to the Nath and Mahanubhava traditions and to the wider
  devotional life of Maharashtra, where Datta Jayanti, marking his birth, is
  celebrated on the full moon of Margashirsha. He is venerated above all as
  the model of the guru who teaches through lived example and unconventional
  wisdom.

regionalVariation: >
  His most intense veneration is in Maharashtra, at shrines including
  Ganagapur, Narsobawadi and Audumbar, and in the wider Deccan, including
  Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh. Some regional traditions treat him purely as
  an avatar of Shiva rather than the full Trimurti together.
