quickIdentity: >
  Venkateswara, widely known as Balaji, is a form of Vishnu worshipped atop
  the Tirumala hills in Andhra Pradesh, believed to have appeared on earth
  to protect humanity through the trials of Kali Yuga.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is called **Balaji**, **Govinda** and **Srinivasa**, names used
  interchangeably by devotees across the temple's vast following, alongside
  the formal name Venkateswara, "Lord of Venkata (hill)."

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  His principal image at Tirumala stands richly ornamented, adorned in gold
  and jewels, in the standard iconography of Vishnu bearing the conch and
  discus, but the specific image and its ornamentation at Tirumala are
  themselves treated as uniquely sacred rather than interchangeable with
  other Vishnu images.

keyStories: >
  Tradition traces the temple's development from around 300 CE, with
  successive dynasties, the Pallavas, Cholas, Pandyas and especially the
  Vijayanagara rulers, expanding and endowing it over the centuries;
  devotional legend holds Venkateswara descended to Tirumala specifically
  to shelter and guide devotees through the hardships of the current cosmic
  age.

familyAndRelations: >
  Worshipped as a form of Vishnu; devotional tradition associates him with
  Padmavati as consort, whose own temple stands nearby at Tiruchanur.

significance: >
  The Tirumala temple is the wealthiest and most visited Hindu temple in
  India, drawing an estimated twenty to twenty-five million pilgrims a
  year, with the tonsure ritual and the temple's laddu prasadam among its
  best-known devotional customs.

regionalVariation: >
  While Tirumala itself is in Andhra Pradesh, his worship as Balaji or
  Srinivasa extends across South India and, through the diaspora, to
  Venkateswara temples built on the same pattern worldwide.
