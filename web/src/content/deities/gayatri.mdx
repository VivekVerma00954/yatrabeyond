quickIdentity: >
  Gayatri is the personification of the Gayatri Mantra, the most revered
  verse of the Vedas, and is honoured as **Vedamata**, the "mother of the
  Vedas." To worship her is essentially to worship sacred sound and knowledge
  given a form.

namesAndEpithets: >
  **Vedamata** ("mother of the Vedas") and **Savitri** (linked to Savitr, the
  Sun as the vivifying power the mantra invokes) are her principal names. She
  is also identified with the sacred verse-metre, the gayatri chhanda, from
  which she takes her name.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She is most often shown as *Panchamukhi*, five-headed, seated on a red
  lotus, with many arms bearing the emblems of the gods. The five faces are
  read as the five vital breaths (pranas) and five elements, and as the four
  Vedas together with the supreme knowledge beyond them; the imagery presents
  her less as a personality than as the ordered totality of Vedic wisdom.

keyStories: >
  Unlike deities with rich narrative cycles, Gayatri's "story" is really the
  story of her mantra: a verse from the Rigveda addressed to the solar Savitr,
  praying that the divine light illumine the intellect. In Puranic tradition
  she is associated with Brahma as the goddess of the Vedas, and at Pushkar
  she is enshrined beside him. Much of her prominence as a distinct goddess is
  comparatively late, growing from meditation on the mantra rather than from
  epic legend.

familyAndRelations: >
  She is associated in Puranic accounts with Brahma the creator, as the
  embodiment of the Vedic knowledge through which he creates. These
  associations are theological rather than narrative, and she is sometimes
  identified or grouped with Saraswati and Savitri as aspects of one goddess
  of sacred learning.

significance: >
  The Gayatri Mantra is recited daily in traditional practice, especially in
  the sandhya rites at dawn and dusk, and is central to the sacred-thread
  (upanayana) initiation. Gayatri as goddess embodies that daily discipline of
  turning the mind toward light and clarity; she is invoked for wisdom, purity
  and spiritual growth.

regionalVariation: >
  In the twentieth century the Gayatri Parivar movement, founded by Shriram
  Sharma Acharya and centred at Shantikunj in Haridwar, made Gayatri worship
  and mantra practice into a large, organised, reformist movement, deliberately
  opening a mantra once guarded by ritual restriction to a much wider public.
  This modern, mass form of Gayatri devotion sits alongside her older, more
  strictly ritual Vedic use.
