quickIdentity: >
  Mangal, or Mangala, personifies the planet Mars as one of the Navagraha and
  rules Tuesday. Also called Lohita, "the red one," he is associated with
  energy, aggression and martial courage.

namesAndEpithets: >
  **Lohita** and **Kuja**, "earth-born," name his fiery colour and his
  mythological origin from the earth goddess Bhumi. He is also called
  **Angaraka**, from *angara*, "ember," another reference to his fiery,
  reddish nature.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is shown with a red body, bloodshot eyes and flaming, curly hair, four
  arms carrying a spear, mace, lotus and trident, and riding a ram, his
  vahana. His hot, bilious temperament in iconography marks him as a
  malevolent yet heroic, combat-skilled figure.

keyStories: >
  Vaishnava tradition holds that he was born as Bhauma, son of the earth
  goddess Bhumi, when Vishnu in his boar (Varaha) avatar raised her from the
  cosmic waters and infused her with his essence. Shaiva tradition instead
  tells that drops of Shiva's sweat, shed while he mourned his wife Sati in
  deep yogic absorption, fell to earth and took the form of a child.

familyAndRelations: >
  Depending on the tradition followed, he is regarded either as the son of
  Vishnu (in his Varaha avatar) and the earth goddess Bhumi, or as born from
  Shiva. Both traditions agree in calling him Bhauma, "son of the earth."

significance: >
  As one of the Navagraha he governs energy, courage, conflict and land or
  property matters in Vedic astrology, and is propitiated especially on
  Tuesdays. His term, mangala, meaning "auspiciousness" in the Rigveda, only
  gained its planetary and astrological sense in later classical Sanskrit
  literature.

regionalVariation: >
  Dedicated Mangal temples and pilgrimage sites exist across India, though
  in smaller number than for deities such as Shani or Surya; medieval
  Sanskrit astronomical texts, from Aryabhata's fifth-century Aryabhatiya
  onward, attest to his long-standing place in Indian astronomical and
  astrological calculation.
