quickIdentity: >
  Vishwakarma, "all-maker," is the divine architect and craftsman of the
  gods, patron deity of workers, artisans, engineers and architects, and
  the mythological builder behind the tools, weapons and cities of the
  Hindu pantheon.

namesAndEpithets: >
  In later mythology he is sometimes identified with **Tvashtr**, an
  earlier Vedic craftsman deity, the two figures merging over time into a
  single divine architect.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is typically depicted as an elderly, wise craftsman bearing tools of
  his trade, a water-pot, a book, a noose and craftsman's instruments such
  as a measuring rod or mason's tools, marking his role as the maker of
  things sacred and useful alike.

keyStories: >
  He is credited with crafting the chariots and weapons of the devas,
  including Indra's thunderbolt, the Vajra, and with building entire
  cities, among them Lanka, Dvaraka and Indraprastha. He is linked to the
  sun god Surya through his daughter Sanjna, Surya's wife: when Sanjna
  could not bear the sun's full radiance, Vishwakarma reduced Surya's
  energy, and the trimmings cast off in the process were fashioned into
  various divine weapons.

familyAndRelations: >
  Father of Sanjna, wife of Surya, tying him by marriage into the solar
  family and, through Sanjna's sons, into the wider mythology of Yama and
  the Ashvins.

significance: >
  Vishwakarma Puja, observed on the 17th or 18th of September, is widely
  celebrated by factory workers, engineers, craftspeople and artisans
  across India, who worship their tools and machinery in his honour on
  this day.

regionalVariation: >
  His worship is especially prominent in eastern India, notably West
  Bengal and Odisha, where Vishwakarma Puja is a major occasion for
  industrial and artisan communities, though his patronage of craft is
  acknowledged in workshops and factories nationwide.
