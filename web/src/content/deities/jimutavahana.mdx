quickIdentity: >
  Jimutavahana is a noble prince of legend, best known from the
  Kathasaritsagara, revered for sacrificing himself to save a stranger's
  child, an act of selflessness that is the founding story behind the
  Jitiya (Jivitputrika) vrat observed by mothers for their children's
  well-being.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He carries no honorific beyond his own name and princely title; his
  significance rests entirely on the single act of self-sacrifice for
  which he is remembered rather than on a wider body of epithets or titles.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He has no standard temple icon; his story rather than his image is
  central to his worship, retold each year as the katha recited during
  the Jitiya vrat rather than depicted in sculpted or painted form.

keyStories: >
  Learning that Garuda, Vishnu's mighty eagle mount, regularly seized and
  devoured young Nagas (serpent beings) as a customary offering, Jimutavahana
  offered his own body in place of the next victim, a young Naga about to
  be sacrificed to save his mother's grief. Moved by this selflessness,
  Garuda relented, sparing both Jimutavahana and the serpents, and the
  episode became a lasting model of compassion and sacrifice for the sake
  of another's child.

familyAndRelations: >
  A prince by birth in the legend's telling; his story is not primarily
  about his own family but about his intervention on behalf of another
  mother's son, which is precisely why mothers invoke him for their own
  children's protection.

significance: >
  His story underlies Jitiya (Jivitputrika), a three-day fast observed in
  the Krishna Paksha of Ashvin by mothers in Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar
  Pradesh and Nepal's Terai region, praying for their children's long life,
  a vrat whose themes of sacrifice and protective motherly love echo his
  own act directly.

regionalVariation: >
  Both the legend and the vrat it underlies are most strongly observed in
  Bihar, Jharkhand and the Terai region of Nepal, with the specific
  telling of the katha varying somewhat between these regions.
