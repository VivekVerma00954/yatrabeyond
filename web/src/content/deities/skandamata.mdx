quickIdentity: >
  Skandamata is the fifth of the Navadurga, Parvati venerated specifically as
  mother of the war-god Kartikeya (Skanda), the goddess's motherhood made
  its own distinct form.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Her name means "mother of Skanda." She is also called **Padmasani**, "she
  who is seated on the lotus," from her usual depiction.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She has four arms and three eyes, light-complexioned, seated on a lotus.
  One hand shows the abhayamudra gesture, another holds her infant son
  Skanda on her lap, and her remaining two hands hold lotus flowers. Her
  mount is a lion.

keyStories: >
  Per the Shiva Purana, after Shiva and Parvati's marriage the gods, led by
  Vishnu and Brahma, travelled to Kailasha to seek Shiva's help against the
  asura Taraka, who could be slain only by a son of Shiva. In the urgency of
  the moment Shiva's seed fell to the ground; Agni, in the form of a dove,
  swallowed it to keep it from being lost, then deposited it with the wives
  of six of the Saptarishi (seven sages) on Shiva's instruction. Unable to
  bear it, they released it as an embryo into the Ganga, which carried it to
  a bed of reeds, where a boy of divine radiance was born. Parvati, furious
  that her privacy had been disturbed, cursed the sages' wives who had
  accompanied their husbands to become barren. The boy, Kartikeya, was born
  on the Shashthi day of Margashirsha, and Parvati nursed and raised him as
  her own, gifting him the divine weapon Shakti; in this nurturing role she
  became known as Skandamata. Kartikeya went on to fulfil his destiny by
  slaying Taraka.

familyAndRelations: >
  Consort Shiva; son Kartikeya (Skanda).

significance: >
  Her day centres on motherhood, nurture and the protective bond between
  mother and child, a markedly different register from the fiercer Navadurga
  forms around her.

regionalVariation: >
  No single named pilgrimage site stands out for her in the sources
  consulted; her worship follows the general pattern of Navratri's
  fifth-night observance.
