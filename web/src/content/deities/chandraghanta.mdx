quickIdentity: >
  Chandraghanta is the third of the Navadurga, worshipped as Shiva's shakti
  in his Chandrashekhara form and venerated as destroyer of demons and
  protector of her devotees, a warrior-goddess who is also a compassionate
  mother figure.

namesAndEpithets: >
  Her name means "she who is adorned with the moon as her bell," from
  *chandra* (moon) and *ghanta* (bell), describing the bell-shaped crescent
  moon on her forehead.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  She has ten arms, bearing a trident, sword, mace, bow and arrow, lotus,
  bell and kamandalu, with one hand in abhayamudra, the gesture dispelling
  fear. She rides a lion, has a golden complexion and a third eye, and her
  seat is held to be the Manipura (navel) chakra.

keyStories: >
  Two stories are told of her. At Shiva and Parvati's wedding, Shiva arrived
  in his wild ascetic form, ash-smeared, garlanded with skulls and snakes,
  riding Nandi at the head of a howling retinue of ghosts and spirits, and
  Parvati's family recoiled in terror; Parvati calmly transformed into the
  radiant, ten-armed Chandraghanta to restore order, and in turn asked Shiva
  to take a gentler form, after which the wedding proceeded. Later, the
  demon Jatukasura raised an army of monstrous bats whose wings blotted out
  the sun and terrorised the world; the gods prayed to Parvati, who again
  became Chandraghanta, and the roar of her lion and the sound of her bell
  scattered the bats before she killed Jatukasura himself with her trident.

familyAndRelations: >
  Consort of Shiva in his Chandrashekhara form.

significance: >
  Her bell's sound is said to have paralysed demons in battle. She is
  understood to combine fierce, battle-ready power with maternal care for
  her devotees, and her worship on Navratri's third night is approached in
  that dual spirit.

regionalVariation: >
  No single named pilgrimage site stands out for her in the sources
  consulted; she is worshipped chiefly as part of the standard nine-night
  Navratri sequence rather than through a distinct regional cult.
