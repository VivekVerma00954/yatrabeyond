quickIdentity: >
  Shukra, "clear" or "bright," personifies the planet Venus as one of the
  Navagraha and rules Friday. He is worshipped as the guru of the asuras and
  is associated with love, beauty, prosperity and the arts.

namesAndEpithets: >
  He is also called **Shukracharya** or **Asuracharya**, "teacher of the
  asuras," a title marking his role as counsellor to the demons in the
  ongoing mythological rivalry between devas and asuras.

iconographyAndSymbols: >
  He is generally depicted as a fair-complexioned, radiant figure, riding a
  chariot or a lotus-drawn carriage, bearing implements associated with
  learning and, in some depictions, a rosary and staff, iconography that
  emphasises his role as a wise and cultivated teacher rather than a warrior.

keyStories: >
  Son of the sage Bhrigu, one of the Saptarishi, he obtained the Sanjeevini
  Vidya, the secret of restoring the dead to life, from Shiva through tapas,
  and used it repeatedly to revive slain asuras. In the story of Vishnu's
  Vamana avatar, Shukra, recognising Vamana's true divine identity, tried to
  stop King Bali's water-pledge by blocking the kamandalu's spout with his
  own body, and was blinded when Vamana pierced it with a blade of grass.

familyAndRelations: >
  Son of the sage Bhrigu. As guru to the asuras he stands as the direct
  counterpart to Brihaspati, guru to the devas, the two forming a
  recurring pair of rival preceptors across Puranic story.

significance: >
  As the Navagraha governing love, beauty, wealth, marriage and the arts,
  he is propitiated especially on Fridays, and is considered, alongside
  Brihaspati, one of the more benevolent grahas in Vedic astrology.

regionalVariation: >
  Like most grahas he is worshipped chiefly as part of the Navagraha temple
  set rather than through numerous standalone shrines, with devotional
  practice centred on Friday observances for prosperity, marriage and
  well-being.
