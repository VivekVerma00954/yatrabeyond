overview: |
  Ballaleshwar Temple in Pali, Raigad district, is one of the eight
  Ashtavinayak shrines (commonly cited as third in the traditional yatra
  sequence, though this varies slightly across sources) and the only one
  named directly after a
  devotee rather than the deity himself, reflecting a distinctive legend
  of childhood devotion at the heart of the site's identity.

history: |
  The temple's origins are traced to an original wooden structure built
  around the 11th century; the temple underwent a significant
  transformation in 1760 under the patronage of Shri Fadnis, a Peshwa-era
  official, giving the site much of its present form.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to legend, a devout boy named Ballal, from the village of
  Pali, would gather his friends to perform elaborate rituals and
  prayers to Ganesha, so absorbed in devotion that his own father,
  displeased, bound him and abandoned him in a forest. Moved by Ballal's
  unwavering faith, Ganesha appeared before him in the guise of a sage,
  freed him, and granted him divine blessing; when Ballal, overcome with
  emotion, asked the deity to remain in Pali permanently, Ganesha
  granted the wish and took residence there, thereafter worshipped as
  Ballaleshwar, "the Lord of Ballal" — giving this shrine its unusual
  distinction of bearing a devotee's name rather than one of Ganesha's
  own traditional epithets.
