overview: |
  Varadvinayak Temple in Mahad, Khalapur taluka, Raigad district, is one
  of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines, distinctive for its swayambhu
  (self-manifested) idol's documented discovery in a nearby lake and for
  a devotional lamp reported to have burned continuously since 1892.

history: |
  The temple's Ganesha idol is recorded as having been found immersed in
  an adjoining lake in 1690 CE; the temple itself was subsequently built
  (or restored) in 1725 CE under the patronage of Peshwa general Ramji
  Mahadev Biwalkar.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Temple legend recounts that the sage Gritsamada undertook penance
  here, pleasing Ganesha, who granted him a boon that his son would be
  undefeated by anyone other than Shiva; Gritsamada in turn asked Ganesha
  to bless the surrounding forest so that devotees who prayed there would
  find success, and requested that Ganesha remain permanently at the
  site, giving the deity his name here, Varadvinayak ("Ganesha who
  grants boons"). The temple's Nandadeep (perpetual lamp), reported to
  have burned without interruption since 1892, is a distinctive
  devotional feature specific to this shrine among the Ashtavinayak.
