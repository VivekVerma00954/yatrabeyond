overview: |
  Ambaji Temple, on the Arasur Hills in Gujarat's Banaskantha district
  near the Rajasthan border, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas,
  traditionally marking the spot where Sati's heart fell. The goddess is
  worshipped here as Arasuri Amba, and — distinctively among the temples
  on this platform — without any idol at all: devotees worship the Shree
  Visa Yantra, a sacred geometric diagram, which remains permanently
  veiled and is never directly visible to the public.

history: |
  Devotional tradition holds that worship at this site predates the
  Vedic period, making Ambaji among the more ancient devotional
  traditions referenced on this platform, though as with several other
  Shakti Peethas, this antiquity is a matter of continuous devotional
  tradition rather than a single archaeologically fixed date. A separate
  legend recounts King Danta's attempt to have the goddess relocate to
  his own kingdom; instructed not to look back while leading her, the
  king could not resist his curiosity, and the goddess — bound by the
  condition of her own agreement — stopped permanently at the spot where
  he looked back, which became the present temple site.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Ambaji's aniconic worship of the Shree Visa Yantra, rather than any
  physical image, places it alongside Jwala Ji as one of this platform's
  Shakti Peethas that deliberately avoids anthropomorphic representation
  of the goddess — both instead centring worship on an abstract or
  natural form understood to more directly express Devi's presence.
  Ambaji is also regarded as one of the four most important Shakti
  Peethas in popular Gujarati and broader Indian devotion.
