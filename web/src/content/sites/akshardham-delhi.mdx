overview: |
  Swaminarayan Akshardham in New Delhi is a major modern Hindu temple and
  cultural campus dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan, built by the
  BAPS Swaminarayan Sanstha. Opened in 2005, it is one of the largest
  Hindu temple complexes in the world and a significant destination
  combining religious worship with cultural and educational exhibitions.

history: |
  The temple was envisioned as early as 1968 by Yogiji Maharaj, the
  spiritual head of BAPS at the time, and was ultimately built under the
  guidance of his successor, Pramukh Swami Maharaj. Construction began
  on 8 November 2000, and the complex was officially opened on 6
  November 2005, two days short of a five-year construction period, in
  a ceremony attended by then-President A. P. J. Abdul Kalam,
  then-Prime Minister Manmohan Singh, L. K. Advani, and the
  then-Lieutenant Governor of Delhi, B. L. Joshi.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The complex is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan (1781-1830), founder
  of the Swaminarayan Sampraday, a Vaishnava-tradition devotional and
  reform movement originating in Gujarat. Beyond its central shrine,
  Akshardham Delhi is notable for combining active worship with an
  extensive cultural exhibition programme (including boat rides, a
  large-format film, and a musical fountain show) depicting Indian
  spiritual and cultural history, giving the complex a distinctive dual
  identity as both an active place of worship and a major
  cultural-tourism destination.
