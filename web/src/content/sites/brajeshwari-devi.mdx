overview: |
  Brajeshwari Devi Temple — also called Vajreshwari, Bajreshwari, or
  Kangra Devi Temple — in Kangra town, is one of the classical 51 Shakti
  Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's left breast fell.
  The goddess is worshipped here as Vajreshwari, "goddess of the
  thunderbolt," a fierce form of Durga.

history: |
  Multiple, overlapping legends attach to this temple's origins. One
  holds that the Pandavas, during their exile, were instructed in a dream
  by Durga to build a temple at Nagarkot (Kangra's historical name) to
  secure victory in the Mahabharata war. A separate mythological legend
  describes the goddess using a divine thunderbolt (vajra) to slay the
  demon Kalikala, giving her the name Vajreshwari. Historically, the
  temple is recorded as having been plundered on multiple occasions by
  the 11th-century raider Mahmud of Ghazni, reflecting its long-standing
  reputation for wealth and sanctity even amid repeated destruction. The
  temple was severely damaged in the major 1905 Kangra earthquake and was
  restored to its present form by 1920.
