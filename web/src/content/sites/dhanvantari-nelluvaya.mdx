overview: |
  Nelluvaya Sree Dhanwanthari Temple, in Nelluvaya village, Thrissur
  district, Kerala, is dedicated to Dhanvantari, the physician of the
  gods and the originator of Ayurveda within Hindu tradition, worshipped
  here as an avatar of Vishnu. It is the only temple in Kerala where Adi
  Dhanwanthari (the primordial, foundational form of the deity) is the
  main enshrined deity.

history: |
  The temple was built in the 18th century through the efforts of
  numerous Ayurvedic physicians from the neighbouring villages of
  Kuttanchery, Wadakkanchery, and Pulamantol, giving it a founding
  history directly rooted in the practising Ayurvedic medical community
  rather than royal or purely devotional patronage. The temple was
  originally administered under the Cochin Devaswom Board before later
  coming under the Thrissur Devaswom Board, and continues to be
  maintained in association with the Vaidyanathan Ayurvedic Samaj.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Dhanvantari is credited in tradition with dividing Ayurveda into eight
  specialised branches (ashtanga ayurveda), including shalya (surgery),
  shalakya, kaya chikitsa, bhutavidya, kaumarabhritya, agadatantra,
  rasayanatantra, and vajikaranatantra, with his own particular
  specialism traditionally held to be shalya chikitsa (surgery). The
  temple is especially known for its Oushada Prasada, called Mukkudi
  Nivedyam, a medicinal offering prepared from dry ginger, pepper,
  pomegranate peel, cumin seed, turmeric, rock salt, and other
  Ayurvedic ingredients, directly connecting the temple's ritual practice
  to its founding community of Ayurvedic physicians.
