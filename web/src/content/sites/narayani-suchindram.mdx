overview: |
  Narayani Temple, within the wider Sthanumalayan Temple complex in
  Suchindram, Kanyakumari district, is one of the classical 51 Shakti
  Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's upper teeth fell.
  The goddess is worshipped here as Narayani, "wife of Narayana"
  (Vishnu), and the presiding Bhairava form is given as Sangharor Samhara.

history: |
  The wider Sthanumalayan Temple complex, of which the Narayani shrine
  forms one part, was constructed in its present form in the 17th
  century, and is notable in its own right as one of the few temples in
  India where Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma are worshipped together in
  combined form (Sthanumalayan, a fused name representing Sthanu/Shiva,
  Mal/Vishnu, and Ayan/Brahma).
