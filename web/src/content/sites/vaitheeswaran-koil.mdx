overview: |
  Vaitheeswaran Koil, near Sirkazhi in Tamil Nadu, is the Angaraka (Mars)
  Navagraha Sthalam, dedicated to Shiva worshipped here as Vaitheeswaran,
  "God of healing," alongside his consort Parvati as Thayyal Nayagi. The
  temple is classified as a Paadal Petra Sthalam, one of the sites
  revered in the 7th-century devotional hymns of the Nayanmar
  (Tamil Saivite saints).

spiritualSignificance: |
  As "God of healing," Vaitheeswaran here is understood to bring physical
  and astrological relief, particularly for devotees affected by mangal
  dosha (an astrological affliction attributed to Mars). The temple's
  sacred tank, Siddhamrutha Theertham, is traditionally credited with
  medicinal and healing properties. The temple is also the recognised
  nerve centre of Naadi Jyotishyam (palm-leaf astrology), a distinctive
  devotional-astrological practice specific to this site, and preserves
  the ashes of Jatayu, the mythological vulture-king of the Ramayana, as
  a further object of reverence.
