history: |
  Vyas Gufa, on the banks of the Saraswati river at Mana village,
  Chamoli district, close to the India-China border and roughly 5.5 km
  from Badrinath, is traditionally held to be the site where sage Vyasa
  composed the Mahabharata, along with the eighteen Puranas and the
  Brahma Sutras. According to legend, Vyasa dictated the Mahabharata to
  Ganesha, who agreed to serve as scribe on condition that Vyasa never
  pause in his recitation; when Ganesha's writing implement broke, he is
  said to have broken off part of his own tusk to continue without
  interruption. A nearby Ganesha cave is associated with this part of
  the legend. A statue of Vyasa is installed and worshipped within the
  cave today.
