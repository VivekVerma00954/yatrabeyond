overview: |
  Swetharanyeswarar Temple in Thiruvenkadu, Mayiladuthurai district, is
  the fourth of the Navagraha Sthalams and is dedicated primarily to
  Shiva, worshipped here as Swetharanyeswarar ("Lord of the white
  forest"), with a distinct subsidiary shrine to Budha (Mercury) making
  it an important pilgrimage centre for astrological remedies related to
  that planet.

history: |
  Stone inscriptions within the temple record substantial contributions
  from the Chola kings Aditya Chola and Rajaraja Chola, giving the site a
  well-documented mediaeval royal patronage history.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Budha is understood in tradition to bestow wisdom and intellect, and
  devotees particularly seek his blessings here for education, artistic
  skill, and relief from Budha Dosham (an astrological affliction),
  typically through worship performed on Wednesdays, the day associated
  with the planet. The temple's ten-day Chariot Festival, held during the
  Tamil month of Thai (mid-January to mid-February), is its most
  significant annual celebration, with the fifth day specifically marked
  by rituals dedicated to Agora Moorthy.
