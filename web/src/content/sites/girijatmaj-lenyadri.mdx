overview: |
  Girijatmaj Temple at Lenyadri, roughly 4.8 km north of Junnar, is one
  of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines and the only one located on a
  mountain and within a rock-cut cave, uniquely merging a Hindu shrine
  with a site of ancient Buddhist monastic heritage. It is also the only
  Ashtavinayak temple where Ganesha is worshipped as a child.

history: |
  The Lenyadri caves comprise roughly 30 rock-cut chambers, archaeologically
  dated to between the 1st and 3rd centuries CE, originally serving as a
  Buddhist vihara (monastery). Cave 7, which originally held 20 monk
  cells, was at some later point adapted into a Ganesha shrine, making
  Lenyadri a distinctive site of layered Buddhist-Hindu religious history
  within a single physical space.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to legend, Parvati (Girija) performed penance in these caves
  for twelve years, seeking a son; pleased by her devotion, Ganesha
  granted her wish and manifested here as her child, giving the site its
  name, Girijatmaj ("son of Girija"), and its distinction as the only
  Ashtavinayak shrine depicting Ganesha specifically as a child rather
  than in his more usual adult or four-armed forms.
