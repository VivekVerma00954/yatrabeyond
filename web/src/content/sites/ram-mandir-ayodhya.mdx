overview: |
  The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya, consecrated in January 2024, is a newly
  built temple dedicated to Rama at the site traditionally identified by
  Hindus as his birthplace (Ram Janmabhoomi). It stands on a site with a
  long and deeply contested modern history: for over four centuries it
  was occupied by the Babri Masjid, a mosque built around 1528–1529, and
  the site's ownership and religious status were the subject of one of
  independent India's most significant and prolonged legal, political,
  and communal disputes.

history: |
  A mosque known as the Babri Masjid was constructed at this site around
  1528–1529. Hindu tradition long held the site to be the precise
  birthplace of Rama, and religious and political tensions around the
  site persisted intermittently over subsequent centuries. In 1949, idols
  of Rama and Sita were placed inside the mosque, an event that
  intensified the dispute and led to the site being locked and contested
  in the courts for decades afterward. In December 1992, a large crowd of
  activists demolished the mosque, an event that triggered serious
  nationwide communal violence and remains one of the most consequential
  and controversial episodes in India's modern history.

  Following an extended legal process, India's Supreme Court ruled in
  November 2019 that the disputed 2.77-acre site be awarded to Hindu
  litigants for the construction of a temple, while separately allocating
  5 acres of land at Dhannipur, elsewhere in Ayodhya district, for the
  Sunni Waqf Board to construct a mosque. A groundbreaking ceremony for
  the temple's construction was performed by Prime Minister Narendra Modi
  in August 2020, with construction overseen by the Shri Ram Janmabhoomi
  Teerth Kshetra trust. The temple was consecrated on 22 January 2024 in a
  ceremony attended by roughly 7,000 guests, with the Prime Minister
  presiding; full construction completion was marked by a further
  ceremony, the Dhwajarohan (flag-hoisting), on 25 November 2025.

spiritualSignificance: |
  For Hindu devotees, darshan of Ram Lalla (the infant form of Rama
  enshrined here) at the traditionally held site of his birth represents
  one of the most significant pilgrimage experiences available within
  Vaishnava devotional practice, given Rama's central place in Hindu
  tradition and the decades-long anticipation surrounding the temple's
  construction. The temple's 2024 consecration was marked by nationwide
  celebration among many Hindus, alongside continued sensitivity around
  the site's history for others; both responses should be understood as
  part of the site's full contemporary significance.

scriptures: |
  Rama's birth in Ayodhya is described in the Ramayana's Bala Kanda.
  The specific identification of this exact site as the precise location
  of that birth is a matter of long-standing devotional tradition and was
  the central contested question in the legal dispute described above,
  rather than a matter of independent archaeological or textual
