overview: |
  Kartik Swami Temple, perched on a narrow ridge above Kanakchauri
  village in Rudraprayag district at nearly 10,000 feet in the Garhwal
  Himalayas, is among the very few dedicated Kartikeya (Murugan)
  temples in North India, a deity far more commonly worshipped across
  South India. Its remote high-altitude setting offers 360-degree
  Himalayan views, including of the Chaukhamba, Neelkanth, and
  Dronagiri peaks.

history: |
  Sources consulted for this page describe the temple as roughly 200

  substantially on its rarity as a dedicated Kartikeya shrine in the
  region, combined with its striking high-altitude location.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's central legend recounts a divine competition set by Shiva
  for his two sons, Ganesha and Kartikeya: whoever completed seven
  circles of the universe first would be worshipped ahead of all other
  deities. Ganesha, celebrated for his wisdom, simply circled his
  parents, declaring them to be his entire universe, and won the
  contest. Kartikeya, feeling wronged by this outcome, renounced the
  world and retreated to the Kronch Parvat (Kronch mountain), where he
  is said to have offered his own bones and flesh to Shiva as a final,
  total act of devotion and sacrifice — the legend from which this
  temple and its remote mountain setting draw their central devotional
  meaning.

  Kanakchauri village, itself roughly 40 km from Rudraprayag town.
