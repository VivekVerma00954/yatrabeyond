overview: |
  Tadkeshwar Mahadev Temple, on a forested hilltop at roughly 2,092 m,
  about 37 km from Lansdowne, Pauri Garhwal district, Uttarakhand, is
  an ancient Shiva shrine set amid dense deodar and pine forest. Its
  name derives from the demon Tarakasura, defeated at this site
  according to local legend, distinguishing it from at least two other
  temples of the same or similar name elsewhere in India.

history: |
  According to legend, Shiva manifested here as a self-formed
  (self-manifested) linga beneath a tall deodar tree, the local term for
  which, "tadka," is said to give the temple its name. Tradition holds
  that the demon Tarakasura meditated and worshipped Shiva at this
  location seeking a boon of invincibility, which Shiva granted with
  the sole exception of Shiva's own son; Kartikeya, Shiva and Parvati's
  son, subsequently fought and killed Tarakasura on this same hilltop.
  Shiva is said to have then attached his own name to the site where
  Tarakasura had performed his penance, giving rise to the temple's
  present name and its promise of continued worship through the
  present age (Kali Yuga).

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's sanctum houses a black stone Shivalinga, roughly two
  feet high, regarded as self-manifested rather than installed. Built
  in traditional Garhwal architectural style using slate, stone, and
  wood, the temple is one of the more visited spiritual sites in this
  part of the Garhwal region, particularly around Maha Shivratri.
