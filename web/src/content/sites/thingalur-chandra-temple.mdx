overview: |
  Thingalur Chandra Temple, also called Kailasanathar Temple, in
  Thingalur near Thanjavur, is the Chandra (Moon) Navagraha Sthalam, one
  of the nine temples of the Navagraha Sthalams circuit in Tamil Nadu.
  While the temple's main presiding deity is Shiva as Kailasanathar, a
  separate image of Chandra, carved in black granite and traditionally
  clad in white cloth, is enshrined in the temple's first precinct.

history: |
  The temple carries historical associations with the devotee Appoothi
  Adikal and the Saivite saint-poet Thirunavukkarasar (Appar), connecting
  it to the wider tradition of the Tevaram-era Nayanmar saints.

spiritualSignificance: |
  During the Tamil months of Puratasi (September-October) and Panguni
  (March-April), the moon's rays are said to fall directly onto the
  temple's presiding deity, a natural phenomenon devotees regard as a
  distinctive marker of the site's connection to Chandra. The temple is
  particularly associated with Chandra Dosha Pariharam, devotional
  remedies sought by pilgrims for emotional imbalance, stress, and mental
  clarity, reflecting Chandra's traditional astrological association
  with the mind.
