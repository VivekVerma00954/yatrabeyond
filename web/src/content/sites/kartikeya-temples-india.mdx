overview: |
  Kartikeya (also called Murugan, Subrahmanya, or Skanda) is worshipped
  predominantly across South India, most systematically through the
  Arupadaiveedu, the six traditional "battle camp" abodes across Tamil
  Nadu: Thiruparankundram, Tiruchendur, Palani, Swamimalai, Thiruthani,
  and Pazhamudircholai. This platform also separately covers Kartik
  Swami Temple in Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand, among the very few
  dedicated Kartikeya shrines anywhere in North India, giving readers a
  contrasting picture of how differently this deity is worshipped across
  the two regions.

history: |
  Each of the seven temples covered here has its own distinct historical
  basis, ranging from Sangam-era Tamil literary references (all six
  Arupadaiveedu shrines) to a comparatively recent, thinly documented
  North Indian shrine (Kartik Swami). See individual site pages for
  detailed history.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The Arupadaiveedu tradition presents Murugan's life and victories as a
  sequential narrative across the six sites: his marriage to Deivanai at
  Thiruparankundram, his defeat of Surapadman at Tiruchendur, his
  renunciate retreat at Palani, his teaching of the Pranava mantra to
  Shiva at Swamimalai, his search for peace and marriage to Valli at
  Thiruthani, and his worship alongside both consorts at Pazhamudircholai.
  Kartik Swami, by contrast, commemorates an entirely different
  narrative moment: Kartikeya's renunciation of the world following his
  contest with Ganesha, reflecting the deity's comparative rarity and
  distinct devotional framing in North Indian tradition.
