  Annapurna Devi Mandir, in the Visheshwarganj area of Varanasi

history: |
  The current temple structure was built in 1729 CE by the Maratha
  Peshwa Baji Rao. The present golden idol of the goddess was
  consecrated through prana-pratishtha rites in 1977 by the
  Shankaracharya of the Sringeri Sharada Peetham, and the temple
  underwent a further Kumbhabhishekam consecration ceremony in February
  2025, some 48 years later, led by Swami Vidhushekhar Bharati,
  designated successor to the Sringeri Shankaracharya.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Annapurna is devotionally understood to ensure that no one in Varanasi
  goes without food, and the temple maintains an active Annadanam (free
  food donation) tradition, serving meals to devotees and the
  underprivileged as a direct expression of this theological role. The
  temple's sanctum houses two images: a brass murti used for daily
  worship, and the golden idol, revealed to the public only once a year
  during the Annakut Mahotsav festival, held the day after Diwali, when
  devotees present the goddess with an especially lavish feast and share
  food with those in need.
