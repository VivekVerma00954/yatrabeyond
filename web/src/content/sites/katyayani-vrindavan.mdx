overview: |
  Katyayani Peeth, in Vrindavan, Mathura district, Uttar Pradesh, is one
  of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally marking the spot
  where the ringlets of Sati's hair fell. The goddess is worshipped here
  as Uma, and Shiva as Bhuteshwar. The temple stands within the same
  Braj/Krishna devotional region covered extensively elsewhere on this
  platform (Banke Bihari, ISKCON Vrindavan, Prem Mandir, Radha Raman, and
  others), giving Vrindavan a notable dual identity as both a major
  Krishna pilgrimage centre and the site of a classical Shakti Peetha.

history: |
  The present temple was constructed in 1923 by Swami Keshavanand
  Brahmachari, a saint devoted to the goddess Katyayani, who is said to
  have built the temple following a dream in which the goddess instructed
  him to come to Vrindavan and establish her worship there.

spiritualSignificance: |

  as a centre where multiple Vaishnava traditions converge, applied here
  within a Shakta context.
