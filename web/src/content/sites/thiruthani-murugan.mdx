overview: |
  Thiruthani Murugan Temple, on a hillock in Tiruvallur district near
  Chennai, is the fifth of the six Arupadaiveedu abodes, revered as
  Shanti Sthalam, the abode of peace, where Murugan is understood to have
  come to calm his anger after his victories over the demon forces
  celebrated at the other abodes.

history: |
  Tamil inscriptions on the temple walls point to construction under the
  Pallava kings in the 9th-10th centuries CE, with later renovation under
  the Cholas. The site's Sangam-era significance is independently
  attested in the Tirumurukāṟṟuppaṭai by the poet Nakkeerar.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to legend, Murugan came to Tiruttani to subside his anger
  after defeating the demon Surapadman elsewhere, and here, in the form
  of a hunter, courted and later married Valli, one of his two consorts.
  The temple's 365 steps (representing the days of the year) and its
  nine-tiered gopuram with four surrounding precincts (praharas) are
  distinctive architectural features; this precise nine-tier gopuram
  iconography is shared with only one other Arupadaiveedu shrine,
  Swamimalai.
