overview: |
  The Ahoi Mata Temple at Radha Kund, in the Braj region of Mathura
  district, Uttar Pradesh, is the most widely referenced site associated
  with Ahoi Mata, a folk goddess worshipped chiefly by mothers seeking
  the well-being and long life of their children. The temple's
  significance is closely bound to Radha Kund itself, one of the most
  sacred bathing tanks in the Braj pilgrimage landscape, and to the
  annual Ahoi Ashtami observance centred there.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Ahoi Mata is worshipped, particularly by mothers, for the protection
  and long life of their children; some traditions identify her with a
  form of Goddess Lakshmi. On Ahoi Ashtami, observed roughly eight days
  before Diwali, women keep a day-long fast and perform puja to Ahoi
  Mata; at Radha Kund specifically, devotees additionally take a sacred
  bath in the kund at Nishita Kaal (around midnight), a practice widely
  associated with blessings for conceiving and safeguarding children.
