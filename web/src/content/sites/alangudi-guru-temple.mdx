overview: |
  Apatsahayeswarar Temple in Alangudi, Thiruvarur district, is the fifth
  of the Navagraha Sthalams, dedicated to Shiva worshipped here as
  Apatsahayeswarar ("Lord who helps in times of distress"), with Guru
  (Brihaspati, Jupiter) enshrined as the temple's specific planetary
  association.

history: |
  The original temple complex is thought to have been built by the Chola
  dynasty, with the present masonry structure attributed to the Nayaks
  of Thanjavur in the 16th century. The temple is mentioned in the
  7th-century devotional hymns of the Nayanmar saint Tirugnana
  Sambandar, giving it Paadal Petra Sthalam status, one of 275 temples
  praised in Saiva devotional literature.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Guru (Brihaspati), the guru of the gods and considered the most
  benefic planet in Hindu astrology, is understood to govern wisdom,
  wealth, faith, and dharma. The temple's name, "Lord who helps in times
  of distress," directly reflects Jupiter's traditional protective and
  beneficial astrological character. Jupiter's periodic transition from
  one zodiac sign to another is marked here with a particularly
  significant festival, distinctive to this temple among the nine
  Navagraha Sthalams.
