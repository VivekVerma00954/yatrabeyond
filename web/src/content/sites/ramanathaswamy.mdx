overview: |
  Ramanathaswamy Temple, on Rameswaram Island in Tamil Nadu, is the
  southernmost of the twelve Jyotirlingas and one of the four Char Dham
  of India (alongside Badrinath, Puri, and Dwarka), giving it a
  distinctive dual status shared with no other Jyotirlinga on this list.
  The temple is famed for having the longest corridor of any Hindu temple
  in India, lined with intricately carved pillars.

history: |
  Tradition holds that Rama, the seventh avatar of Vishnu, worshipped
  Shiva at this site after his war against Ravana in Lanka, to atone for
  the killing of Ravana, who was himself a Brahmin by birth — paralleling
  the same "sin of killing a Brahmin" theme found in the Chandrashila/
  Tungnath legend in the Panch Kedar circuit. The lingam here is
  traditionally said to have been established and worshipped by Rama
  before he crossed to Lanka via the causeway known as Rama Setu (Adam's
  Bridge).

  The temple's principal shrine sanctum was renovated in the 12th century
  under the Pandya dynasty and later by monarchs of the Jaffna kingdom
  (Sri Lanka). The current structure, including its famous corridors, is
  generally dated to around the 17th century, with substantial patronage
  and expansion from royal families of Travancore, Ramanathapuram, Mysore,
  and Pudukkottai over the following centuries.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Ramanathaswamy's combined status as both a Jyotirlinga and one of the
  four Char Dham gives it a uniquely central place in pan-Indian Hindu
  pilgrimage geography, connecting the Ramayana's narrative of Rama's
  penance with the broader Shaivite Jyotirlinga tradition. Ritual bathing
  at the temple's 22 sacred wells (tirthams), each said to have distinct
  properties, is a significant part of the pilgrimage practice here,
  alongside bathing in the sea at Agni Tirtham nearby.

scriptures: |
  The Rama-Ravana atonement narrative draws on the Ramayana. The site's

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to February offers the most comfortable
  coastal weather, avoiding the intense summer heat of this southern
  coastal region.

  **Getting there:** Rameswaram is connected to the mainland by the Pamban
  road and rail bridges; Madurai is the nearest major airport
  (approximately 170 km), with Rameswaram also having its own railway
  station.

  **The 22 tirthams:** Ritual bathing at the temple's 22 wells is a
  distinctive part of the pilgrimage; priests at the temple typically
  guide this process for visiting pilgrims.
