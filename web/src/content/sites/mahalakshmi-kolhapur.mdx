overview: |
  Mahalakshmi Temple, in Kolhapur, Maharashtra, also known locally as
  Ambabai Temple, is dedicated to the goddess Mahalakshmi. **This
  platform found genuine, unresolved disagreement across sources over
  Kolhapur's exact classification.** It is most consistently identified
  as one of the eighteen Maha Shakti Peethas described in the Ashta Dasa
  Shakti Peetha Stotram attributed to Adi Shankaracharya (Sri Pitham,
  associated there with an eye), and as one of the "three and a half"
  Shakti Peethas recognised within a distinct regional Maharashtrian
  tradition, alongside Tuljapur (Bhavani), Mahur (Mahamaya Renuka), and
  Saptashringi (Jagadamba, also covered on this platform). At least one
  further composite source list, however, also includes Kolhapur
  directly within the classical 51 itself, naming the goddess there as
  Mahishasuramardini and the body part as the third eye, a third,
  independent attribution beyond the two already noted. This platform
  presents all of these claims rather than asserting any single one as
  definitively settled.

history: |
  The temple is dated to 634 CE, built by the Chalukya king Karnadeva,
  reflecting Chalukya architectural style — making it one of the older,
  more securely dated temple structures referenced on this platform. A
  local legend holds that the demon Kolhasura, who tormented the gods and
  other beings, was destroyed by Mahalakshmi at Karavira (Kolhapur's
  ancient name), and that the site of his death became a sacred tirtha,
  with the goddess subsequently taking up permanent residence in the
  shrine that forms the core of the temple today.
