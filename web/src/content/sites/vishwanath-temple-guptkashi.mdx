overview: |
  Vishwanath Temple in Guptkashi, Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand, is
  an ancient Shiva temple whose name, Guptkashi ("Hidden Kashi"),
  reflects local tradition holding the town's spiritual standing next
  only to Varanasi (Kashi) itself. The town lies on the approach road
  to Kedarnath and is a common overnight stop for pilgrims travelling
  onward to the shrine.

history: |
  According to legend, after the Kurukshetra war, the Pandavas sought
  out Shiva to seek forgiveness for the killing that had taken place
  during the war, but Shiva avoided them, choosing to remain concealed
  in this region before ultimately appearing to them at Kedarnath; this
  legend of Shiva's concealment gives the town its name, Guptkashi
  ("Hidden Kashi"). A separate, distinct tradition holds that when the
  Mughal emperor Aurangzeb had the original Kashi Vishwanath Temple at
  Varanasi demolished in 1669 CE, the temple's Shivalinga was moved here
  for safekeeping, and that it has remained at Guptkashi since; this is
  a strong and specific historical claim that requires independent
  verification before publication, given its significance and the
  existence of other claims regarding the fate of the original Varanasi
  linga.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Alongside the main Vishwanath shrine, the temple complex includes a
  separate shrine to Ardhanarishvara, the composite half-Shiva,
  half-Parvati form, with a metal Nandi statue positioned facing the
  main Shiva image. The town's devotional standing, understood locally
  as second only to Varanasi, and its position on the Kedarnath
  pilgrimage route, give it a distinctive dual identity as both an
  independent pilgrimage destination and a practical staging point for
  the wider Kedarnath yatra.
