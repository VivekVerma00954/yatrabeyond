overview: |
  Panchsagar Shakti Peeth, at the Varahi Devi Temple in Lohaghat,
  Champawat district, Uttarakhand, is one of the classical 51 Shakti
  Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's lower teeth (or,
  in some tellings, her navel) fell. The goddess is worshipped here in
  the distinctive form of Varahi, depicted with the head of a female
  boar, with Shiva present as Maharudra. This platform notes that at
  least two other locations, Danteshwari Mandir in Chhattisgarh and a
  Varahi temple in Devidhura, Uttarakhand, are also separately claimed
  as this same Panchsagar Shakti Peetha in different source lists, a
  multi-location dispute this platform flags rather than resolves.
