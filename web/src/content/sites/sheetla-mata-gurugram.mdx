overview: |
  Sheetla Mata Mandir in Gurugram (Gurgaon), Haryana, is dedicated to
  Sheetla Mata (also called Mata Masani), a folk-Shakti goddess
  particularly venerated for her power to heal, historically associated
  specifically with protection from smallpox and more broadly with skin
  ailments and children's health.

history: |
  Devotional tradition connects Sheetla Mata with Guru Dronacharya, the
  teacher of the Pandavas and Kauravas in the Mahabharata, describing her
  as his wife who devoted herself to caring for the sick, particularly
  children suffering from smallpox; after her death, villagers built a
  temple in her honour, and she came to be worshipped as Mata Sheetla or
  Mata Masani. The present temple structure is credited to the 18th
  century Jat king Jawahar Singh of Bharatpur, built to commemorate his
  victory over the Mughals, a victory for which he had sought Sheetla
  Mata's blessing beforehand.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Sheetla Mata is understood as an embodiment of Shakti specifically
  associated with healing, and her intercession is particularly sought
  for protection from disease, historically smallpox and today more
  broadly skin conditions and childhood illnesses. Sheetla Ashtami, marking
  her divine appearance, is the temple's principal annual festival. The
  temple also maintains a langar (free community meal) tradition, serving
  all visitors regardless of caste, creed, or social status.
