overview: |
  Mangla Gauri Temple, on Mangalagauri hill on the banks of the Phalgu
  river in Gaya, Bihar, is one of the Ashtadasha (18) Maha Shakti
  Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's breast fell.
  Since then, the presiding goddess, Mangala Gauri, an incarnation of
  Parvati, has been worshipped here as a symbol of nourishment.

history: |
  The present temple structure dates to the 15th century, though the
  temple is referenced across multiple much older texts, including the
  Padma Purana, Vayu Purana, Agni Purana, and Devi Bhagavata Purana,
  indicating substantial devotional recognition of the site well before
  the current building's construction.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Gaya's much wider significance as one of Hinduism's most important
  sites for performing pinda daan (ancestral rites for departed souls)
  gives Mangla Gauri's presence here a devotional context markedly
  different from most other Shakti Peethas on this platform, which are
  not typically associated with ancestor-worship traditions. Every
  Tuesday, women observe a dedicated fast at the temple, offering
  bangles, fruits, and sweets in prayer for family prosperity and their
  husbands' wellbeing.

scriptures: |
