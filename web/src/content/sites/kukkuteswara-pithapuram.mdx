overview: |
  Puruhutika Devi Temple, within the wider Kukkuteswara Swamy Temple
  complex in Pithapuram, Andhra Pradesh, is one of the Ashtadasha (18)
  Maha Shakti Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's back
  fell. The presiding deity of the wider complex, Kukkuteswara, is a
  form of Shiva depicted as a rooster, paired with his consort
  Rajarajeswari Devi; Puruhutika Devi's own shrine sits on the same
  premises. Pithapuram's name is said to derive from the site's earlier
  names, "Puruhoothikapuram" and later "Peetikapuram."

history: |
  The site came under Chola influence between the 9th and 12th
  centuries, integrated at that time into the Vengi kingdom, with
  temple construction and endowments reflecting South Indian
  architectural patronage typical of the period. By the 15th century,
  the Telugu poet Srinatha referenced the temple in his Bheemeswara
  Puranamu, describing it as one of four moksha sthanas (sites of
  liberation) in the Andhra region.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Pithapuram is also known as Pada Gaya Kshetram, one of the Trigaya
  Kshetrams (three sacred Gaya sites for performing ancestral rites),
  and is described in some traditions as equivalent in this specific
  ritual role to Varanasi, referred to here as Dakshina Kasi. This gives
  Puruhutika Devi's Shakti Peetha a further, independent devotional
  layer tied to ancestor veneration, distinct from though complementary
  to its core Shakti Peetha identity, a pattern similar to Biraja
  Temple's own dual Shakti Peetha/Nabhi Gaya significance, also covered
  on this platform.

scriptures: |
  Puruhutika's identification within the Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Peetha
  tradition is documented in the Ashta Dasa Shakti Peetha Stotram
  attributed to Adi Shankaracharya; the temple's moksha sthana status is
  referenced in Srinatha's 15th-century Bheemeswara Puranamu. Specific
