overview: |
  Siddhivinayak Temple at Siddhatek, on the banks of the Bhima river in
  Ahmednagar district, is one of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines (sources
  vary on its exact numbered position in the traditional yatra sequence,
  most commonly cited as second or third) and the only Ashtavinayak
  shrine located in Ahmednagar district. It is distinctive among
  the Ashtavinayak for depicting Ganesha with his trunk turned to the
  right rather than the left, a feature traditionally associated with
  greater ritual strictness and potency.

history: |
  Temple tradition holds that the original shrine here was established
  by Vishnu himself, following the legend recounted below; over the
  following centuries the temple underwent multiple reconstructions, with
  the present structure's grandeur owed substantially to Ahilyabai
  Holkar, the 18th-century Queen of Indore renowned for her extensive
  temple restoration work across India (also documented elsewhere on
  this platform in connection with other sites she supported).

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to legend, Vishnu undertook rigorous penance at this site,
  chanting the mantra "Om Sri Ganeshaya Namah," and Ganesha, pleased by
  his devotion, granted Vishnu the divine power (siddhi) needed to
  vanquish the demons Madhu and Kaitabha — giving the site its name,
  Siddhatek ("the hill of accomplished power"), and the deity's title,
  Siddhivinayak ("Ganesha who grants spiritual accomplishment"). The
  temple's Jaya-Vijaya brass gatekeeper sculptures, more typically
  associated with Vishnu's own shrines, flank the central Ganesha icon
  here, a distinctive cross-Vaishnava devotional detail reflecting the
  Vishnu-penance legend.
