overview: |
  Nartiang Durga Temple, also known as Jainteshwari Mandir, on a hillock
  in Nartiang, West Jaintia Hills district, Meghalaya, is one of the
  classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where
  Sati's left thigh fell. It is one of only three Shakti Peethas in
  northeast India, alongside Kamakhya (Assam, covered elsewhere on this
  platform) and Tripura Sundari (Tripura, also covered on this
  platform). The goddess here is called Jayanti, and her Bhairava
  Kamadishwar.

history: |
  Roughly 600 years ago, King Manik made Nartiang the summer capital of
  the Jaintia Kingdom; according to temple tradition, the goddess
  appeared to him in a dream and instructed him to establish a temple in
  her honour, leading to the founding of the Jainteshwari temple.

spiritualSignificance: |
  A genuine location dispute surrounds this Shakti Peetha: alongside the
  Nartiang site, competing claims place the true Jayanti Shakti Peetha at
  Jaintiapur in Bangladesh (the historical Jaintia Hills kingdom capital
  before it was divided by the India-Bangladesh border), and at Amta in
  West Bengal, where a goddess is worshipped as Maa Melai Chandi. This
  platform notes all three claims rather than asserting one as
  definitively settled, consistent with the genuinely unresolved
  historical record.
