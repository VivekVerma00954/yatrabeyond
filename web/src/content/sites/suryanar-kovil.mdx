overview: |
  Suryanar Kovil, near Aduthurai between Kumbakonam and Mayiladuthurai in
  Thanjavur district, Tamil Nadu, is the anchor temple of the Navagraha
  Sthalams, the set of nine temples across the Kumbakonam-Mayiladuthurai
  region, each dedicated to one of the nine Navagraha (planetary
  deities). Suryanar Kovil is the only standalone temple among the nine
  dedicated specifically to Surya, and uniquely also contains subsidiary
  shrines to all eight of the other Navagraha deities within its own
  complex, arranged facing the central Surya shrine.

history: |
  The present masonry structure is generally dated to the reign of the
  Chola king Kulottunga Choladeva (1060-1118 CE), in the 11th century,
  with later additions made during the Vijayanagara period. The temple
  features a five-tiered rajagopuram (entrance tower) and a rectangular
  compound layout, with the central Surya shrine raised on an elevated
  platform.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to temple legend, the sage Kalava suffered from severe
  ailments, including leprosy, and prayed to the Navagrahas for relief;
  pleased by his devotion, the nine planetary deities cured him, but
  Brahma, angered that the planets had exercised powers he felt were not
  theirs to grant, cursed the Navagrahas themselves with leprosy and
  banished them to earth at Vellurukku Vanam (a jungle of white wild
  flowers), the site now occupied by this temple complex. This legend
  underlies the site's role as the central anchor of the wider Navagraha
  Sthalams pilgrimage circuit, with devotees typically visiting Suryanar
  Kovil either first or last when completing all nine temples.
