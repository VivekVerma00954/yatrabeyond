history: |
  Art-historical research (notably by Michael Brand) traces organised
  worship of Santoshi Mata to the early 1960s, when five geographically
  dispersed temples dedicated to her were established across northern
  India; the Jodhpur temple is among this original small group. Santoshi
  Mata's devotional profile expanded dramatically nationwide following
  the release of the 1975 Bollywood film "Jai Santoshi Maa," which
  dramatised her story and that of her devotee Satyavati, propelling the
  previously little-known goddess into the wider pan-Indian Hindu
  devotional landscape and prompting the incorporation of her images and
  shrines into temples across the country.
