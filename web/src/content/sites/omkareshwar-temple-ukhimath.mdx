overview: |
  Omkareshwar Temple, in Ukhimath, Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand,
  serves as the winter seat of the Kedarnath deity. Each year, when
  Kedarnath Temple closes for the winter (after Diwali) due to heavy
  snow, the Utsav Doli (processional idol) of Kedarnath is carried down
  in a formal procession to Omkareshwar Temple, where all daily rituals
  and worship continue for the following six months (roughly November
  to April), before the deity is carried back up to Kedarnath as the
  temple reopens.

history: |
  Local tradition holds that the temple's name derives from the
  penance of the sage Mandhata, who is said to have stood on one leg
  for twelve years at this spot to appease Shiva, who then appeared to
  him in the form of "Omkar," the primordial sound. The town name,
  Ukhimath, is said to derive from "Ushamath," referencing a further
  Puranic tradition holding this as the site of the wedding of Usha,
  daughter of the demon king Banasura, and Aniruddha, grandson of
  Krishna.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Beyond hosting Kedarnath's winter worship, the temple also receives
  the deity from Madmaheshwar (Madhyamaheshwar), one of the Panch Kedar
  temples, during the same winter closure period, giving Ukhimath a
  distinctive dual role as winter seat for two separate major Shaiva
  shrines. This makes the temple a significant pilgrimage destination in
  its own right during the winter months, when Kedarnath itself is
  inaccessible.
