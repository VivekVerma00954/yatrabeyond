history: |
  Tulsi Manas Mandir was constructed in 1964 near Durga Kund, at a site
  historically associated with Goswami Tulsidas's composition and
  recitation of the Ramcharitmanas in the 16th century. Unlike most
  temples, its walls are inscribed with the full text of the
  Ramcharitmanas, carved into marble, and the complex includes a museum
  housing a collection of related manuscripts and artefacts.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Tulsi (holy basil) holds a distinctive status in Hindu devotional
  practice as a plant regarded as sacred in her own right, personified
  as a goddess and traditionally considered inseparable from Vishnu
  worship; the annual Tulsi Vivah ceremony, performed between
  Prabodhini Ekadashi and Kartik Purnima, ceremonially marries the Tulsi
  plant to Vishnu (represented as Shaligram, or as Krishna or Rama), and
  is widely performed at household Tulsi Vrindavan altars across India
  rather than at any single temple. The Varanasi temple's dedication to
  Tulsidas, rather than to Tulsi devi directly, reflects a related but
  distinct devotional lineage sharing the same name.
