overview: |
  The Ashtavinayak ("eight Ganeshas") is a traditional pilgrimage circuit
  of eight self-manifested (swayambhu) Ganesha temples across Pune,
  Raigad, and Ahmednagar districts in Maharashtra: Moreshwar/Mayureshwar
  (Morgaon), Siddhivinayak (Siddhatek), Ballaleshwar (Pali),
  Varadvinayak (Mahad), Chintamani (Theur), Girijatmaj (Lenyadri),
  Vighnahar (Ozar), and Mahaganapati (Ranjangaon). Each temple's idol is
  distinct in form and legend, and completing the pilgrimage to all
  eight (the Ashtavinayak Yatra) is a major devotional undertaking within
  the Ganapatya tradition, which reveres Ganesha as the Supreme Being.
  This platform also separately covers Siddhivinayak Temple in Mumbai,
  a distinct and much-visited modern temple that shares its deity form's
  name with, but is not part of, the classical eight-temple Ashtavinayak
  circuit.

history: |
  The eight temples have widely varying individual histories, from
  ancient rock-cut Buddhist caves later adapted for Hindu worship
  (Lenyadri) to sites substantially developed under Peshwa-era patronage
  in the 18th century (Ozar, Theur, Ranjangaon). Sources vary on the
  precise traditional sequence in which the middle six temples (beyond
  the universally agreed starting point of Morgaon) should be visited;
  this platform presents each temple's individually cited common
  position while noting that exact sequencing varies across pilgrimage
  operators and sources.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The Ashtavinayak Yatra is traditionally begun and ended at Morgaon,
  and completing all eight temples in a single continuous pilgrimage is
  considered a significant devotional accomplishment. Each temple's
  distinctive legend, and its associated idol form (from Ballaleshwar's
  devotee-named shrine to Girijatmaj's cave-set child form to
  Siddhatek's uniquely right-turned trunk), together illustrate the wide
  devotional range within Ganapatya worship of a single deity across
  just eight closely clustered sites.
