overview: |
  Tripura Sundari Temple, in Udaipur (also called Rajbari), Gomati
  district, Tripura, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas,
  traditionally marking the spot where Sati's right foot fell. The
  presiding goddess, Tripura Sundari (also called Tripureshwari), is the
  fourth of the ten Mahavidyas, giving this site a dual identity as both
  a classical Shakti Peetha and a major Mahavidya shrine. This platform
  notes a separate site, also named Tripura Sundari Temple, in Banswara,
  Rajasthan, which appears in some source lists as a distinct additional
  Shakti Peetha claim; the two temples are unrelated beyond sharing the
  same goddess name.
