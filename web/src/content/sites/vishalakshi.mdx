overview: |
  Vishalakshi Temple, in Varanasi, is one of the classical 51 Shakti
  Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where Sati's earring (and, in
  some tellings, her face) fell. Dedicated to Vishalakshi, "the one with
  wide, compassionate eyes" — an aspect of Parvati — the temple stands in
  close devotional relationship with the city's own Kashi Vishwanath
  Temple, together representing, in local tradition, the union of Shiva
  and Shakti within Varanasi's sacred geography.

history: |
  The temple is referenced in ancient texts including the Skanda Purana
  and the Kashi Khanda, indicating a long history of devotional
  recognition at this site. The temple as it stands in more recent
  centuries has been built and maintained since the 19th century by the
  Nattukottai Nagarathars, a mercantile community originally from Tamil
  Nadu — reflecting the broader pattern, seen at several sites across
  this platform, of South Indian merchant and religious communities
  investing in North Indian temple maintenance far from their own home
  region.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Many scholars and devotees understand Vishalakshi's presence alongside
  Kashi Vishwanath as symbolically completing the divine union of Shakti
  and Shiva within Varanasi, one of Hinduism's most sacred cities — a
  pairing that has shaped both the religious life and cultural identity
  of the city more broadly, beyond the specific devotional practice at
  either individual temple.
