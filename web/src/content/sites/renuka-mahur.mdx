overview: |
  Renuka Devi Temple, in Mahur (also called Mahurgad), Nanded district,
  Maharashtra, is one of the Ashtadasha (18) Maha Shakti Peethas and one
  of Maharashtra's own "three and a half" regional Shakti Peethas,
  alongside Mahalakshmi (Kolhapur) and Saptashrungi (Nashik), both
  covered elsewhere on this platform, and Tuljapur (Bhavani). This
  platform found a genuine cross-source body-part discrepancy for this
  site: Wikipedia's Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Pitha table attributes Sati's
  left hand to Mahur (naming it Moola Pitham), while other sources
  instead describe Sati's head as having fallen here. This platform
  notes both rather than asserting one as settled. The temple is
  dedicated to Renuka, mother of Parashurama (an avatar of Vishnu) and
  wife of the sage Jamadagni.

history: |
  The present temple is dated to the 12th century, believed to have been
  built by a Yadava king of Devagiri roughly eight or nine hundred years
  ago, featuring intricately carved stone walls and pillars. Mahur was
  traditionally known as "Matripura" or "Matapur," reflecting its
  long-standing significance as a centre of goddess worship independent
  of the specific 12th-century structure.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Renuka's identity as the mother of Parashurama, the sixth avatar of
  Vishnu, gives this Shakti Peetha a devotional link to the wider
  Vaishnava avatar tradition alongside its primary Shakta identity, a
  dual significance shared with relatively few of this platform's other
  Shakti Peethas. The nearby Dattatreya Temple, on a separate hill within
  Mahur, gives the wider site an additional, distinct devotional layer
  connected to the Datta tradition.

scriptures: |
  Renuka's identification within the Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Peetha
  tradition is documented in the Ashta Dasa Shakti Peetha Stotram
  attributed to Adi Shankaracharya; her identity as Parashurama's mother
  draws on the Mahabharata and various Puranas. Specific verse-level
  citation requires expert verification before publication.
