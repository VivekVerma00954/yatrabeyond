overview: |
  Vighnahar Temple (also called Vigneshwara Temple) in Ozar, near
  Junnar, is one of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines and the only one
  among them featuring a golden dome (Sonyacha Kalash) and pinnacle, a
  distinctive architectural feature tied directly to its Peshwa-era
  patronage.

history: |
  The present temple structure dates to the Peshwa era, generally given
  as 1785 CE. Chimaji Appa, brother and military commander of Peshwa Baji
  Rao I, is credited with renovating the temple and covering its shikhara
  (spire) with gold following his 1739 capture of Vasai Fort from the
  Portuguese, connecting the temple's most distinctive feature directly
  to a specific documented military victory. The temple was further
  rebuilt in 1967 under Appashastri Joshi.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The Ganesha form worshipped here, Vigneshwara ("Lord of obstacles") or
  Vighnahar ("Remover of obstacles"), is associated with the legend of
  Ganesha's defeat of the demon Vignasura, who is said to have disrupted
  sacred rituals and good deeds across the universe by creating
  constant obstacles; Ganesha's victory over this specific demon gives
  the deity his name and role here as protector against obstruction more
  broadly.
