overview: |
  Bijli Mahadev Temple, at roughly 2,460 m in Kashawri village, Kullu
  Valley, Himachal Pradesh, is an ancient Shiva shrine believed to be
  over 1,000 years old, distinctive for a tall wooden staff (locally
  called the Dhuj) fixed atop the temple, traditionally said to attract
  lightning that strikes the shrine's Shivalinga.

history: |
  One local legend holds that the temple commemorates Shiva's defeat of
  the demon Jalandhara, whose trident strike saved the Kullu valley from
  being submerged; a related version names the demon Kulanta, who took
  the form of a giant serpent threatening to flood the region before
  being defeated by Shiva. The temple's precise founding date is not
  well documented beyond its widely cited estimated age of over 1,000
  years.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's defining feature is its association with lightning: a
  tall single-trunk deodar staff, tipped with a muslin-wrapped silver
  coin, is traditionally believed to attract Shiva's blessing in the
  form of a lightning strike, said locally to occur roughly once every
  twelve years (with some popular retellings describing it as an annual
  event). Each strike is said to shatter the temple's Shivalinga into
  pieces, which the resident priest then reassembles using a paste of
  butter and sattu (roasted gram flour) collected from local
  households, restoring the linga to its original form amid prayers and
  chanting.

scriptures: |
  The Jalandhara/Kulanta demon-defeat legends and the lightning-linga
  restoration tradition are documented through regional oral and
  devotional tradition rather than classical Puranic scripture. The
  frequency of the lightning strikes themselves (variously described as
  roughly every twelve years or, in some accounts, more frequently)
  should be treated as devotional tradition rather than a verified
  natural phenomenon pending further research.
