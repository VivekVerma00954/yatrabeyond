overview: |
  Siddhivinayak Temple, in the Prabhadevi area of central Mumbai, is one
  of the city's most visited and wealthiest temples, drawing devotees
  from across all walks of Mumbai life, including many from the city's
  film and political establishment. This platform notes that this
  temple is distinct from Siddhivinayak Temple at Siddhatek, one of the
  eight classical Ashtavinayak shrines covered elsewhere on this
  platform; the two share a name and deity form but are separate sites
  with separate histories.

history: |
  The temple was built on 19 November 1801 by Laxman Vithu and Deubai
  Patil, a childless couple who founded it in the hope that the deity
  might grant children to other women unable to conceive, giving the
  temple a founding story rooted in personal devotional need rather than
  royal or institutional patronage. The original structure was a modest
  brick building measuring roughly 3.6 by 3.6 metres with a small
  dome-shaped shikhara; the temple underwent a major renovation in 1990,
  transforming the 200-year-old shrine into the considerably larger and
  more elaborate structure seen today.

spiritualSignificance: |
  "Siddhivinayak" translates as "Ganesha who fulfils all wishes and
  aspirations," and the idol here is depicted with its trunk curved to
  the right, a form traditionally associated with "Siddhapeeth" status,
  understood to mean the deity here responds unusually quickly to
  devotees' prayers. The temple is one of the richest temple trusts in
  Mumbai, reportedly receiving between roughly ₹100 million and ₹150
  million in donations annually, and is especially closely associated
  with Mumbai's film industry, drawing many well-known devotees from
  Bollywood alongside the wider public.
