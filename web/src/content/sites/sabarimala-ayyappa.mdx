overview: |
  Sabarimala Sree Dharma Sastha Temple, atop a forested hillock roughly
  3,000 feet above sea level in Kerala's Pathanamthitta district, is
  dedicated to Ayyappa (also known as Dharma Sastha) and is one of the
  largest annual pilgrimage gatherings in the world, drawing an estimated
  10 to 15 million pilgrims each year during its main season.

history: |
  While oral tradition and legend suggest worship at Sabarimala predates
  written history, with some accounts placing the temple's founding as
  far back as roughly 800 years ago, the earliest confirmed written
  record of the temple dates only to the early 19th century (around
  1818). This platform notes the gap between the traditional oral dating
  and the documented written record as a genuine historical uncertainty
  rather than a settled fact.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Ayyappa is understood in tradition as the divine son of Devasena and
  the union of Shiva and Vishnu (in Vishnu's Mohini form), giving the
  deity a distinctive theological position bridging Shaivism and
  Vaishnavism. Sabarimala is believed to be the site where Ayyappa
  meditated after defeating the demon Mahishi. Devotees undertake a
  41-day Vratham (vow of ritual abstinence) before the pilgrimage,
  observing a simple vegetarian lifestyle, celibacy, and wearing black or
  blue clothing, and traditionally walk barefoot, with the pilgrimage
  itself explicitly framed as transcending barriers of caste and creed.
  The temple's 18 golden steps (Pathinettam Padi) are a particularly
  significant devotional threshold pilgrims ascend at the pilgrimage's
  culmination.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** The main pilgrimage season is the roughly 60-day
  period beginning in the Malayalam month of Vrishchikam (November-
  December), encompassing the 41-day Mandala Kaalam and the subsequent
  Makaravilakku festival; the temple is closed outside these designated
  opening periods, so current opening dates must be checked before
  planning any visit.

  **Getting there:** Pilgrims reach the base at Pamba by road, then trek
  the remaining distance to the temple itself; Pathanamthitta district is
  reached by road from Kochi or Thiruvananthapuram.
