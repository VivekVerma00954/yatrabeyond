overview: |
  Sapt Rishi Ashram, on the banks of the Ganga at Sapt Sarovar in
  Haridwar, Uttarakhand, is the most widely cited site associated with
  the Sapta Rishi (the seven sages) in the Hindu tradition, traditionally
  held to be the place where the seven sages meditated and where the
  Ganga is said to have split into seven streams so as not to disturb
  their contemplation.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to tradition, seven sages, commonly listed as Kashyapa,
  Vashisht, Atri, Vishwamitra, Jamadagni, Bharadwaja, and Gautama,
  meditated at this site but were disturbed by the thunderous flow of
  the Ganga; to allow them to continue in peace, the river is said to
  have divided herself into seven smaller streams, giving the site its
  name, Sapt Sarovar (seven pools/lakes). Rishi Panchami, a festival
  honouring the seven sages, is associated with this broader tradition.
  The ashram today combines an active place of worship and meditation
  with community and educational facilities, including a Sanskrit
  school and residential accommodation.
