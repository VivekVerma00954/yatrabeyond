overview: |
  Yamunotri, in Uttarakhand's Uttarkashi district, is the traditional
  starting point of the Char Dham Yatra of Uttarakhand and one of its
  four sites (alongside Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath). At 3,293 m,
  the temple is dedicated to the goddess Yamuna, sacred as the second most
  important river in Hindu tradition after the Ganga. The river's actual
  glacial source lies much further away and higher, at roughly 6,387 m
  near the Bandarpunch peaks, and is not itself part of the standard
  pilgrimage; the temple marks the accessible point of devotional
  significance rather than the literal source.

history: |
  The present temple was built in 1839 by Sundarshan Shah, then king of
  Tehri, one of the Garhwal region's princely states. Yamuna is described
  in Hindu tradition as the daughter of Surya (the sun god) and the sister
  of Yama, the god of death — a relationship that gives ritual bathing in
  the Yamuna a particular devotional significance, understood by many
  pilgrims as offering protection from an untimely or painful death,
  reflecting the goddess's close mythological kinship with Yama himself.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Beside the temple, Surya Kund, the most significant of Yamunotri's hot
  water springs, is used for a distinctive ritual practice: pilgrims
  traditionally cook rice and potatoes in cloth pouches submerged in the
  spring's near-boiling water, then take this cooked offering (prepared by
  the spring's natural heat rather than a conventional fire) as prasad. A
  large rock beside Surya Kund, called Divya Shila, is worshipped before
  Yamuna herself as part of the standard ritual sequence. A separate,
  cooler spring, Gauri Kund, is used for bathing, being tepid rather than
  scalding.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** The temple opens and closes annually following a
  calendar similar to the other Char Dham of Uttarakhand, typically late
  April/May to October/November; May–June and September–October offer the
  most stable weather. Yamunotri is the traditional first stop of the
  Char Dham Yatra sequence.

  **Getting there:** Reached by road to Janki Chatti (via Barkot), the
  last motorable point, followed by an approximately 3 km trek or
  pony/palanquin ride to the temple, typically taking around 3 hours on
  foot.

  **Rituals at Surya Kund:** Cooking rice or potatoes in the hot spring
  water as prasad is a distinctive part of the pilgrimage; local guidance
  at the site typically explains the process for first-time visitors.

  serving Char Dham pilgrim traffic; Yamunotri itself, at the end of the
  short trek, has more limited facilities, and most pilgrims day-trip
  from Janki Chatti rather than staying overnight at the temple itself.
