overview: |
  Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, in Mathura, marks the site traditionally
  held by Hindus to be the precise birthplace of Krishna, within what is
  described in devotional tradition as a former prison cell of the tyrant
  king Kansa, Krishna's maternal uncle. As at Ayodhya's Ram Janmabhoomi,
  the site carries a complex and, in this case, still-unresolved modern
  legal history involving an adjoining mosque, the Shahi Idgah, built in
  the 17th century.

history: |
  Temple tradition holds that a shrine at this site was originally
  established by Vajranabh, traditionally Krishna's great-grandson, with
  the site rebuilt or restored multiple times over subsequent centuries —
  temple accounts describe as many as five successive reconstructions.
  Hindu accounts state that a substantial temple built in 1618 was
  demolished under the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, who ordered construction
  of the Shahi Idgah mosque at the site around 1670.

  The site's modern ownership history is well documented: the land was
  auctioned by the East India Company in 1815 and purchased by Raja
  Patnimal; it was later acquired in 1944 by industrialist Jugal Kishore
  Birla, and placed under the Shri Krishna Janmabhoomi Trust in 1951. A
  1968 compromise agreement between the Shri Krishna Janmasthan Seva
  Sangh and the Shahi Idgah mosque committee granted the temple land to
  the Trust while confirming the mosque committee's authority over the
  adjoining mosque, with the Seva Sangh relinquishing any legal claim over
  the mosque itself.

  **The current dispute:** in 2020, a group of petitioners filed a
  lawsuit challenging the validity of the 1968 compromise, arguing the
  Shahi Idgah mosque occupies land belonging to the Krishna Janmabhoomi
  Trust and seeking its removal. In December 2023, the Allahabad High
  Court ordered a court-monitored survey of the mosque. As of the most
  recent sources reviewed for this page, the matter remained under
  active litigation, with no final resolution reached — distinct from the
  Ayodhya Ram Janmabhoomi dispute, which was resolved by the Supreme
  Court in 2019. Current litigation status must be verified before
  publication, and this platform presents the above as a documented,
  still-unfolding legal timeline rather than a settled matter, without
  taking a position on the underlying claims of either party.

scriptures: |
  Krishna's birth in Mathura, within Kansa's prison, is described in the
  Bhagavata Purana's tenth canto and the Harivamsa. The specific
  identification of this exact site as the precise location of that birth
  is a matter of long-standing devotional tradition and is the central
  contested question in the ongoing legal dispute described above, rather
  than a matter of independent archaeological or textual consensus.
