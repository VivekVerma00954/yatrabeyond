overview: |
  Nagapooshani Amman Temple, on the island of Nainativu near Jaffna,
  Sri Lanka, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally
  marking the spot where the silambu (anklets) of Sati fell. The
  presiding goddess, Nagapooshani (also identified as Bhuvaneshwari), is
  paired with Shiva as Rakshaseshwar (also called Nayanair). Adi
  Shankaracharya, in the 9th century, is separately credited with
  identifying this temple as one of the 64 Shakti Peethams in the
  Shakti Peetha Stotram tradition, and it is referenced in the
  Brahmanda Purana.

history: |
  The ancient temple structure was demolished by Portuguese colonial
  forces in 1620; the present structure was rebuilt between 1720 and
  1790. The temple complex today houses four gopurams (gateway towers),
  the tallest, the eastern Raja Raja Gopuram, standing at 108 feet, and
  contains an estimated 10,000 sculptures across the refurbished
  complex.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Nagapooshani has been referenced in Tamil literature since antiquity,
  appearing in classical works including Manimekalai and Kundalakesi,
  giving this Shakti Peetha a documented literary history stretching
  back considerably further than its most recent physical
