overview: |
  Khatu Shyam Ji Temple, in Khatu town, Sikar district, Rajasthan, is one
  of the most heavily visited pilgrimage sites in North India, dedicated
  to Khatu Shyam Ji, identified with Barbarika, grandson of the Pandava
  Bhima and son of Ghatotkacha, from the Mahabharata narrative.

history: |
  According to legend, the present temple traces to 1027 CE, when local
  ruler Roop Singh Chauhan was guided in a dream to unearth a buried
  idol identified as Barbarika's head at a site now called Shyam Kund, a
  sacred pond near the temple. The temple was substantially rebuilt in
  1720 CE by Deewan Abhaya Singh, and the present structure, built with
  white Makrana marble in traditional Rajasthani style, reflects this
  later reconstruction.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to the Mahabharata-derived legend, Barbarika possessed three
  divine arrows capable of single-handedly determining the outcome of the
  Kurukshetra war; to preserve the war's fairness, Krishna, disguised as
  a Brahmin, tested Barbarika's resolve and ultimately asked him to give
  his own head in an act of supreme charity (dana). Barbarika complied,
  and Krishna, moved by this sacrifice, granted him the boon that he
  would be worshipped in the present age (Kali Yuga) under the name
  Shyam. This narrative of unconditional sacrifice and devotion underlies
  the temple's very large contemporary following, and the annual Phalgun
  Mela draws millions of pilgrims.

scriptures: |
