overview: |
  Shiv Bari Temple, also known as Drona Shiv Mandir, stands amid forest
  land at Ambota village near Gagret town, Una district, Himachal
  Pradesh. Local tradition holds the site to be several thousand years
  old and connects its founding directly to Guru Dronacharya, the
  Mahabharata-era teacher of the Pandavas and Kauravas.

history: |
  According to local legend, Guru Dronacharya resided at Ambota village
  and, after bathing in the nearby Swan river, would journey towards the
  Himalaya to worship Shiva. A further tradition holds that Yayati,
  described in this local telling as Dronacharya's daughter, undertook
  devoted chanting and penance at this spot, and that Shiva, pleased
  with her devotion, appeared and agreed to remain here in the form of a
  linga; Dronacharya is then said to have built a temple and installed
  the Shivalinga, described locally as a Pindi (rounded stone) form.
  A separate local historical account holds that the Mughal emperor
  Aurangzeb attempted to have the Pindi excavated during an attack on
  the temple, and that swarms of red insects emerged from the stone and
  drove off his soldiers, after which the emperor is said to have
  surrendered and apologised before Shiva.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's Shivalinga is worshipped in Pindi (rounded stone) form,
  alongside idols locally described as ancient representations of
  Virbhadra, Kartikeya (Swami Kartikey), Kubera, and Ganesha. Maha
  Shivratri is marked with a large annual fair at the temple, drawing
  significant local devotion tied to the Dronacharya legend and the
  temple's long-held local reputation for antiquity.
