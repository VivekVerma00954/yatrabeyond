overview: |
  Gayatri Mata Temple, atop Nilgiri Hill in Pushkar, Rajasthan, is
  dedicated to Gayatri, understood in local tradition as a secondary
  consort accepted by Brahma to complete his yagna at Pushkar, and
  devotionally identified with the Gayatri Mantra itself, one of the
  most widely recited verses in Hindu tradition. The temple sits within
  the same devotional landscape as this platform's existing Brahma
  Temple, Pushkar page, directly connected to the same founding legend.

history: |
  Local tradition places the temple's construction at roughly 500 years
  ago, though this dating is not independently well documented; the
  temple's present, more modern structure is generally attributed to the
  early 20th century. Notably, the temple is said to house idols of both
  Gayatri and Savitri dating to as early as the 7th century CE, an
  antiquity considerably older than the visible structure itself housing
  them.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to the Pushkar founding legend (recounted more fully on this
  platform's Brahma Temple, Pushkar page), Brahma's principal consort
  Savitri was delayed in arriving for his yagna; to avoid the ritual's
  auspicious moment passing, Brahma married a maiden named Gayatri,
  purified and presented to him by Indra from a local cowherd family, so
  the ceremony could proceed. This act is central to the wider Pushkar
  curse narrative involving Savitri's anger at being displaced, discussed
  on this platform's Savitri Mata Temple and Brahma Temple pages. The
  temple offers panoramic views over Pushkar and its lake from its
  Nilgiri Hill setting.
