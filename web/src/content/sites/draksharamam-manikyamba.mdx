overview: |
  The Bhimeswara Swamy Temple at Draksharamam, East Godavari district,
  Andhra Pradesh, houses the Manikyamba shrine, one of the Ashtadasha
  (18) Maha Shakti Peethas. Sources disagree on the exact body part:
  most identify Sati's left cheek, though at least one source instead
  cites her navel; this platform notes both rather than asserting either
  as settled. The temple complex is also one of the five Pancharama
  Kshetras, a distinct regional Shaiva pilgrimage circuit, giving
  Draksharamam a dual identity as both a major Shiva and Shakti site.

history: |
  Draksharamam's founding legend connects the site to King Daksha's own
  yajna, the same sacrificial ceremony whose disruption by Sati's death
  set the entire Shakti Peetha tradition in motion; the temple's stone
  construction reflects a blend of Chalukya and Chola architectural
  styles, with a two-storeyed mandapa and two enclosing parakaras
  (courtyards).

spiritualSignificance: |
  Draksharamam's combined identity as both a Pancharama Kshetra (Shiva)
  and an Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Peetha (Shakti) places it, alongside
  Srisailam's Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Temple also covered on this
  platform, among a small number of sites where major Shaiva and Shakta
  pilgrimage traditions converge at the same physical location.

scriptures: |
  Manikyamba's identification within the Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Peetha
  tradition is documented in the Ashta Dasa Shakti Peetha Stotram
  attributed to Adi Shankaracharya. Specific verse-level citation
