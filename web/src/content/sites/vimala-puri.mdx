overview: |
  Vimala Temple, within the walls of the great Jagannath Temple complex
  in Puri, is one of the **Four Adi Shakti Peethas**, alongside Kamakhya,
  Kalighat, and Tara Tarini, all covered elsewhere on this platform.
  Accounts differ on the exact body part: most sources associate Vimala
  with Sati's feet (Pada Khanda), consistent with the Puranic naming
  Pada Pitha, while some local Puri tradition instead attributes her
  navel to this site. This platform notes both claims rather than
  asserting one as settled.

history: |
  According to the Madala Panji, the palm-leaf chronicle of the
  Jagannath Temple, Vimala Temple was built in the 6th century by Yayati
  Keshari of the Somavanshi dynasty, though the temple's surviving
  architecture is more consistent with 9th-century Eastern Ganga
  construction, suggesting a destruction and rebuilding at some point
  between these two periods, a detail this platform flags as requiring
  expert reconciliation rather than a single confirmed date.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Vimala's placement inside the Jagannath Temple complex, one of the
  most visited Vaishnava pilgrimage sites in India, gives her worship a
  distinctive dual character: Shakta devotees regard her as Shakti to
  Jagannath's Shiva, while Vaishnava devotees regard her as Lakshmi to
  Jagannath's Vishnu. In either framing, Vimala is treated as the
  effective guardian of the temple complex, and prasad offered at the
  main Jagannath shrine is only considered true Mahaprasad after first
  being offered to Vimala, a ritual sequencing that gives this Shakti
  Peetha an unusually central, structurally embedded role within a much
  larger, globally known pilgrimage site.

scriptures: |
  Vimala's identification as one of the Four Adi Shakti Peethas is
  documented in the Devi Bhagavata Purana and Kalika Purana; her
  construction history draws on the Madala Panji chronicle. Specific
  verse-level citation requires expert verification before publication.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to February offers the most comfortable
  weather; visiting during the Ratha Yatra (typically June or July)
  offers a unique view of Vimala's role in the wider festival sequence,
  though this is also the most crowded time to visit.

  **Getting there:** Puri has its own railway station and is well
  connected by road from Bhubaneswar, which has the nearest airport.
