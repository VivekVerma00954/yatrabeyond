overview: |
  Sitamarhi, in Bihar, is traditionally revered as the birthplace of
  Sita, and the Janaki Mandir at Punaura Dham marks the specific site
  where, according to the Ramayana narrative, King Janaka discovered the

  location.

history: |
  According to tradition, King Janaka found Sita as an infant emerging
  from the earth at Punaura, near present-day Sitamarhi, while ploughing
  a field as part of a ritual yagna to seek rain; the site was later
  marked with a tank (Janaki-kund) and, after Sita's marriage to Rama,
  with stone images of Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana. Over time the specific
  location is said to have been overtaken by jungle, before being
  rediscovered roughly 500 years ago by a Hindu ascetic named Birbal Das
  of Ayodhya, who is credited with clearing the site, finding Janaka's
  original images, and re-establishing worship there. The temple
  structure standing at Punaura Dham today is comparatively recent,
  generally dated to around a century old.
