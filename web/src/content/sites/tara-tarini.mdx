overview: |
  Tara Tarini, on a hill above the Rushikulya river near Purushottampur in
  Odisha's Ganjam district, is one of the **Four Adi Shakti Peethas**
  (Chatasrah Aadi) explicitly named in the Devi Bhagavata Purana and
  Kalika Purana as the oldest and most powerful of all Shakti sites,
  alongside Kamakhya, Kalighat, and Vimala (Puri), all three of which are
  also covered on this platform. Tara Tarini marks the spot where Sati's
  breasts (Stana Khanda) fell, and the goddess is worshipped here in twin
  form, as the sister deities Tara and Tarini, rather than as a single
  image.

spiritualSignificance: |
  As one of only four sites accorded Adi (foundational) status among the
  full network of Shakti Peethas, Tara Tarini occupies an unusually
  concentrated place in Shakta theology: the twin goddesses are
  worshipped without any consort Bhairava image at the main shrine, an
  arrangement distinct from most other Shakti Peethas covered on this
  platform, which typically pair the goddess with a specific Bhairava
  form.
