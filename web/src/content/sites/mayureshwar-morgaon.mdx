overview: |
  Mayureshwar Temple (also called Moreshwar Temple) in Morgaon, Pune
  district, is the first of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines and
  traditionally the prescribed starting and ending point of the complete
  Ashtavinayak Yatra pilgrimage circuit. Morgaon is regarded as the
  foremost centre of the Ganapatya sect, which venerates Ganesha as the
  Supreme Being, and its self-manifested (swayambhu) Ganesha idol here
  is worshipped as Mayureshwar, "Lord of the Peacock."

history: |
  While some traditional accounts trace the site's origins to the 9th or
  10th century CE, closer historical analysis suggests the standing
  temple structure likely dates no earlier than the 17th century; the
  temple's prominence grew substantially through the influence of Moraya
  Gosavi, a revered Ganapatya saint whose devotional lineage shaped
  Ashtavinayak worship across the wider region.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Mayureshwar's name and iconography derive from a legend in which
  Ganesha rode a peacock (mayura in Sanskrit, mora in Marathi) to defeat
  the demon Sindhura, giving the deity his distinctive name at this
  site. The idol is depicted with four arms, three eyes, and his trunk
  turned to the left, holding a noose and elephant goad, with a lower
  hand resting on his knee holding a modaka (sweet). As the sect's
  foremost devotional centre, Morgaon carries particular weight within
  the wider Ganapatya theological tradition beyond its role as merely
  the first Ashtavinayak stop.
