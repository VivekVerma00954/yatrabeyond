overview: |
  The Brahma Temple (Jagatpita Brahma Mandir) in Pushkar, Rajasthan, is
  the most prominent temple in the world dedicated to Brahma, the
  creator deity of the Hindu Trimurti. Standing beside the sacred
  Pushkar Lake, it is popularly described in scripture and tradition as
  the only Brahma temple in the world, a claim tied to a specific curse
  narrative involving Brahma's consort.

history: |
  The temple's founding legend attributes it to the sage Vishwamitra,
  built following a yagna (fire ritual) performed by Brahma at Pushkar.
  The 8th-century philosopher Adi Shankaracharya is credited with
  renovating the temple, and the structure seen today reflects later
  medieval additions attributed to Maharaja Jawat Raj of Ratlam, while
  retaining its original design.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Hindu tradition holds that Brahma was cursed by his consort Savitri
  (in some tellings, identified with Saraswati) to never be worshipped
  in temples, after he proceeded with a yagna at Pushkar in her absence
  by taking Gayatri as a substitute consort for the ritual; the curse was
  said to have been softened to permit worship of Brahma specifically at
  Pushkar, making this temple the singular exception to an otherwise
  near-total absence of dedicated Brahma worship across India. The
  temple's sanctum enshrines Brahma alongside Gayatri, and Kartik
  Poornima draws especially large crowds of pilgrims who bathe in
  Pushkar Lake before visiting the temple.
