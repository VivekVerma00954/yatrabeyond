overview: |
  Bakreshwar Temple, on the banks of the Paaphra river in Birbhum
  district, West Bengal, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas and,
  by several accounts, one of the most powerful among West Bengal's
  unusually dense cluster of thirteen such sites. It marks the spot
  where the portion of Sati's forehead between her eyebrows fell, said
  to represent the goddess's "mind." The presiding image, Mahishmardini
  (destroyer of the demon Mahishasura), is protected by the Bhairava
  Vakranath.

history: |
  While devotional tradition holds the site sacred since ancient times,
  the temple's present structure dates to 1761 CE, when it was rebuilt
  by King Darpanarayan, a patron also associated with the nearby
  Kiriteswari Temple's 19th-century reconstruction.
