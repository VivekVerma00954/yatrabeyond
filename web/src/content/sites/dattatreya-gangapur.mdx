overview: |
  Shri Kshetra Gangapur Dattatreya Temple, on the banks of the Bhima
  river in Kalaburagi (Gulbarga) district, Karnataka, is one of the most
  significant Dattatreya Peethas in India, associated with Sri Narasimha
  Saraswati Swami, revered within the Datta Sampradaya as the second
  major incarnation of Dattatreya in the current Kali Yuga (the first
  being Sripada Sri Vallabha).

history: |
  Narasimha Saraswati Swami is recorded as having been born in Lad
  Karanja, Maharashtra, in the 14th century, and is said to have spent
  the last twenty years of his life at Gangapur. The temple's central
  Nirgun Math structure is generally dated to the 15th century. A
  distinctive and continuously documented tradition holds that the
  temple's daily Maha Naivedya (food offering ritual) has not been missed
  on a single day since the 16th century, reportedly continuing
  uninterrupted even through the COVID-19 pandemic.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Dattatreya is understood in Hindu tradition as a combined incarnation
  of the Trimurti (Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva together), and Gangapur is
  regarded as a Guru Sthan (seat of spiritual teaching), attracting
  devotees drawn specifically to Narasimha Saraswati's teaching lineage
  within the wider Datta Sampradaya. The temple's uninterrupted, multi-
  century daily offering tradition is itself a significant devotional
  claim central to how the site is understood by pilgrims.
