overview: |
  The Dakshayani Shakti Peetha, also called Manasa Shakti Peeth, on the
  banks of Lake Manasarovar near Mount Kailash in Tibet, is one of the
  classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where
  Sati's right palm fell. Unlike almost every other Shakti Peetha
  covered on this platform, there is no temple structure or carved image
  here at all: a single large boulder, lying at extreme altitude on the
  lake's southwestern shore, is the object of worship. Shiva is present
  in this form as Amar ("Immortal").
