overview: |
  Thirunallar Saneeswaran Temple (also called Dharbaranyeswarar Temple),
  in Thirunallar, Karaikal district of the Puducherry Union Territory, is
  the Shani (Saturn) Navagraha Sthalam, and is distinctively regarded as
  the only temple where Shani is said to have lost his power of
  affliction by the grace of Shiva, giving it a uniquely significant
  place among the nine Navagraha Sthalams for devotees seeking relief
  from Shani Dosha.

history: |
  The site was originally named Dharbaranyam, a forest with a flourishing
  growth of Dharba grass; the Shiva lingam enshrined here is said to
  still bear the impression of Dharba grass, offered as evidence of this
  origin. Temple legend recounts that the legendary King Nala was freed
  from the malefic effects of Shani Dosha after worshipping here and
  bathing in the temple's tank, subsequently named Nalan Theertham in
  his honour.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Thirunallar is understood as the pre-eminent site for relief from
  Shani Dosha, the astrological affliction attributed to Saturn's
  malefic influence, on the basis that Shani himself is held to have
  relinquished his own power to cause harm at this specific location out
  of reverence for Shiva. Bathing in the Nalan Theertham tank before
  worship is considered central to the devotional practice here,
  believed to wash away afflictions and misfortunes tied to past karma.
