overview: |
  Triyuginarayan Temple, in Triyuginarayan village, Rudraprayag
  district, Uttarakhand, is traditionally held to be the site of the
  celestial wedding of Shiva and Parvati, witnessed by Vishnu. A
  perpetual sacred fire, said to have burned since the wedding itself,
  continues to burn in a havan-kund (fire pit) before the temple, giving
  it its alternate name, Akhand Dhuni ("eternal flame") temple.

history: |
  Tradition holds that the temple was built by Adi Shankaracharya. The
  temple's architectural style is said to resemble that of the
  Kedarnath Temple. A stone in front of the temple, known as the Brahma
  Shila, is regarded as marking the exact spot of the divine wedding.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to legend, Shiva and Parvati's wedding took place here
  during the Satya Yuga, with Vishnu formally officiating the ceremony
  and acting as Parvati's brother, while Brahma served as priest, in
  the presence of the perpetual sacred fire that continues to burn
  before the temple today. This distinctive legend has made
  Triyuginarayan an increasingly popular contemporary wedding
  destination for couples seeking Shiva's blessing for their own
  marriages, alongside its standing as a pilgrimage site connected to
  the wider Kedarnath route.
