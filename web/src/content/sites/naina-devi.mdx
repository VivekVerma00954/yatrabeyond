overview: |
  Naina Devi Temple, on a hilltop in Bilaspur district overlooking Govind
  Sagar Lake, is one of the classical Shakti Peethas — most sources place
  it among the 51, though it is occasionally cited within an alternate
  108-part enumeration — traditionally marking the spot where Sati's
  right eye fell. The name "Naina" (eyes) directly reflects this
  association.

history: |
  Devotional tradition holds that the site has been sacred since Vedic
  times, though the temple structure itself is more commonly dated to
  construction in the 15th century, with its present idols installed in
  1842 by a devotee named Moti Ram Shah. A large, centuries-old Peepal
  tree within the temple complex is a notable, long-standing feature of
  the site.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Naina Devi is also known as Mahishapeeth, referencing a separate legend
  in which the goddess defeated the buffalo-demon Mahishasura and
  removed both his eyes, prompting the gods to exclaim "Jai Naina" in
  celebration — giving the site an additional layer of significance
  beyond the core Sati-dismemberment narrative, directly tying the
  "eyes" theme to two independent legends at once.

scriptures: |
  Naina Devi's identification with Sati's eye draws on Shakti Peetha
  Stotram literature; the Mahishasura/Mahishapeeth legend draws on the
  Devi Mahatmya tradition found in the Markandeya Purana. Specific
  verse-level citation requires expert verification before publication.
