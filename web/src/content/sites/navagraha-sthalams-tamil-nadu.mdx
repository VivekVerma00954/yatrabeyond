overview: |
  The Navagraha Sthalams are a set of nine temples in the
  Kumbakonam-Mayiladuthurai region of Tamil Nadu (with one, Thirunallar,
  just across the border in the Karaikal district of Puducherry), each
  associated with one of the nine Navagraha (planetary deities) of Hindu
  astrology: Suryanar Kovil (Surya/Sun), Thingalur (Chandra/Moon),
  Vaitheeswaran Koil (Angaraka/Mars), Thiruvenkadu (Budha/Mercury),
  Alangudi (Guru/Jupiter), Kanjanur (Sukra/Venus), Thirunallar
  (Shani/Saturn), Thirunageswaram (Rahu), and Keezhaperumpallam (Ketu).
  With the sole exception of Suryanar Kovil, each temple's primary
  presiding deity is Shiva (worshipped under a locally specific name),
  with the associated Navagraha deity enshrined in a distinct subsidiary
  shrine within the same complex.

history: |
  Most of the nine temples have documented histories extending back to
  the Chola period (roughly 9th-13th centuries CE), with several
  additionally recognised as Paadal Petra Sthalams, sites honoured in the
  7th-century Tevaram hymns of the Tamil Nayanmar saints, giving the
  wider circuit a devotional history considerably predating its more
  specific codification as a nine-temple Navagraha pilgrimage.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Devotees traditionally undertake the full nine-temple circuit seeking
  relief from doshas (astrological afflictions) attributed to
  unfavourable planetary positions in their individual horoscopes,
  typically visiting each temple on the day of the week traditionally
  associated with its planetary deity. Suryanar Kovil, the only
  standalone Surya temple among the nine and uniquely also housing
  subsidiary shrines to all eight other Navagraha deities within its own
  complex, is commonly treated as the natural starting or ending point
  of the circuit.
