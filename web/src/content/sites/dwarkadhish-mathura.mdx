overview: |
  Dwarkadhish Temple, in Mathura near the Yamuna riverbank ghats, is
  dedicated to Krishna worshipped in his kingly form as Dwarkanath (or
  Dwarkadhish, "Lord of Dwarka"), alongside Radha as Radharani. This
  Mathura temple is entirely distinct from the much larger, older
  Dwarkadhish Temple in Dwarka, Gujarat, one of the pan-Indian Char Dham —
  the two share a name and deity identity but are separate temples with
  independent histories, and this platform's pages should never be
  conflated with one another.

history: |
  The present temple structure was built in 1814 (some sources cite
  1815) by Seth Gokul Das Parikh, treasurer of the Gwalior State under the
  Scindia dynasty and a devoted follower of Krishna, who was moved to
  construct a grand temple in Mathura honouring Krishna's kingly form.
  The temple belongs to the Pushtimarg tradition, founded by the
  16th-century saint Vallabhacharya, and is built in Rajasthani
  architectural style, richly decorated with carvings and paintings
  depicting scenes from Krishna's life.

spiritualSignificance: |
  As one of Mathura's most visited and architecturally distinctive

  focus of nearby Krishna Janmabhoomi. Its riverside location near the
  Yamuna ghats also connects it to the broader tradition of ritual
  bathing in the Yamuna as part of a Braj pilgrimage.
