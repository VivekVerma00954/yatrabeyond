overview: |
  Kalpeshwar is the fifth and final temple of the traditional Panch Kedar
  circuit, and the only one of the five that remains open to pilgrims all
  year round — a direct consequence of its comparatively low altitude,
  around 2,200 m, in the Urgam Valley of Chamoli district. Tradition holds
  it to enshrine the **matted hair (jata)** of Shiva, completing the
  fivefold body-part legend shared across the Panch Kedar. It is also, by
  a wide margin, the easiest of the five to reach.

history: |
  Kalpeshwar shares the Panch Kedar founding legend with the other four
  temples (see the Panch Kedar overview page for the full account): after
  Shiva evaded the Pandavas by taking bull form and sinking into the
  earth, his hair is said to have surfaced here, completing the sequence
  that begins with the hump at Kedarnath. As with the other Panch Kedar,
  the temple is traditionally attributed to the Pandavas, with no
  independently verified construction date.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Because it is reachable in any season and without a demanding trek,
  Kalpeshwar occupies a distinctive place in the Panch Kedar tradition: for
  pilgrims unable to undertake the harder treks to Rudranath or
  Madhyamaheshwar, or visiting outside the narrow open season of the other
  four, it is often the one Panch Kedar site they are able to complete.
  Some pilgrims specifically visit in winter, when the other four are
  closed and their deities have been carried down to valley shrines,
  making Kalpeshwar the only place to receive a Panch Kedar darshan at
  that time of year.

scriptures: |
  As with the other Panch Kedar, the founding legend draws on the
  Pandavas' post-Kurukshetra narrative from the **Mahabharata**, elaborated
  in regional material associated with the **Kedara Khanda** of the
  **Skanda Purana**. No specific verse-level citation for Kalpeshwar's
  individual legend has been confirmed; treat the summary above as
  devotional tradition pending expert verification.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** Open all year, unlike the other four Panch Kedar.
  March–June and September–November offer the most comfortable weather;
  winter access is possible but expect cold conditions and check road
  status to Joshimath/Helang, which can be affected by snow at higher
  points on the approach even though the temple itself stays open.

  **Getting there:** Joshimath is the base town. From there, approximately
  1.5 hours by road to Helang, then about 12 km by jeep or shared taxi to
  Urgam village, followed by an easy 2.5 km walk to the temple — described
  by multiple sources as the easiest approach of any Panch Kedar site.

familySuitability: |
  Well suited to families, including young children and older visitors.
  The walk from Urgam is short and gentle compared to any other Panch
  Kedar site, and the year-round access removes the seasonal planning
  constraints that apply to the other four.

accommodation: |
  Joshimath has a full range of hotels and guesthouses, being a major hub
  town for the wider Char Dham and Panch Kedar region. Urgam village has
  more limited, basic guesthouse or homestay options. Most visitors base
  themselves in Joshimath and day-trip to Kalpeshwar.

localServices: |
  Joshimath offers the fullest range of services in the Panch Kedar
  region — food, medical facilities, banking, and reasonable mobile
  connectivity. Urgam and the immediate approach to the temple have only
  basic facilities; carry cash and check connectivity expectations before
  the final approach.

preservation: |
  Kalpeshwar's lower altitude and year-round accessibility mean it avoids
  the acute seasonal-crowding pressure seen at Kedarnath, but the wider
  Urgam Valley and Joshimath corridor face broader regional environmental
  concerns common to the Garhwal Himalaya, including land subsidence
  issues that have affected Joshimath itself in recent years and are the
  subject of ongoing government and geological assessment.
