overview: |
  The Lakshman Temple stands on the bank of the Ganga at Lakshman Jhula in
  Rishikesh, Uttarakhand, and is dedicated to Lakshmana, the younger brother
  and lifelong companion of Rama. It is among the best-known temples
  specifically associated with Lakshmana, drawing pilgrims and visitors who
  cross to it beside the famous footbridge that shares his name. The temple is
  noted for its old wall sculptures and paintings and for its riverside
  setting in one of North India's principal centres of pilgrimage and yoga.

history: |
  Local tradition holds that this is a spot where Lakshmana performed
  penance and meditation, and that he crossed the Ganga here, an association
  that gives the adjacent bridge its name. The original Lakshman Jhula was a
  rope bridge; it was rebuilt in iron and steel and opened in 1939, and for
  decades was one of Rishikesh's most recognisable landmarks. The historic
  bridge was later closed to the public on structural-safety grounds, with a
  modern replacement crossing planned nearby. The precise founding date of the
  temple itself is not firmly documented in the accessible sources and should
  be verified with temple or local heritage authorities before publication.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple honours Lakshmana as the embodiment of devoted service (seva)
  and loyalty, the brother who chose fourteen years of forest exile at Rama's
  side over comfort at home. Its riverside location joins that devotion to the
  sanctity of the Ganga herself, so a visit typically combines darshan at the
  temple with time on the ghats. It sits within the wider sacred landscape of
  Rishikesh, long regarded as a gateway to the Himalayan pilgrimage routes and
  a centre of spiritual practice.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** February to April and September to November offer the
  most comfortable weather; the monsoon (roughly July to September) can bring
  heavy rain and a swollen, fast river. Ram Navami is the most relevant
  festival, given the Rama connection.

  **Getting there:** Rishikesh is well connected by road; the nearest airport
  is Dehradun (Jolly Grant) and the nearest major railheads are Rishikesh and
  Haridwar. From central Rishikesh, the Lakshman Jhula area is a short local
  drive or auto-rickshaw ride.

  **Getting around:** The temple is reached on foot in the Lakshman Jhula
  area. Because the historic bridge's public access has changed, confirm the
  current pedestrian route across or along the river before setting out.
