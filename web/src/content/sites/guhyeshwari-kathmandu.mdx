overview: |
  Guhyeshwari Temple, on the southern bank of the Bagmati river about
  1 km east of the Pashupatinath Temple in Kathmandu, Nepal, is one of
  the classical 51 Shakti Peethas. Its name, from the Sanskrit *guhya*
  (secret) and *ishwari* (goddess), reflects both the temple's
  historically restricted, esoteric worship and genuine cross-source
  disagreement over the exact body part: sources variously cite Sati's
  hips, her anus, or her yoni. This platform notes all three claims
  rather than asserting one as settled, consistent with the article's
  own account of significant textual confusion on this specific point,
  including a caution not to conflate Guhyeshwari with a separately
  named Nepal Mahatmya tradition in which Sati's knees, not this site,
  fell at the confluence of the Vishnumati and Bagmati rivers.

history: |
  King Pratap Malla renovated the temple in the 17th century. The
  Varahi Tantra separately describes the wider "Guhya Mandala" of Nepal
  as carrying merit a hundred times greater than most other sacred
  sites, and states that no Shakti Peetha exceeds Guhyeshwari in
  importance, reflecting the site's unusually elevated status within
  Tantric tradition specifically.
