overview: |
  While Ayodhya is Rama's home city and the site of his birth and reign,
  devotion to Rama across India extends through a number of independently
  significant temples associated with other episodes of his life, later
  legends of devotion, or purely local traditions of his worship. This
  overview brings together five such sites covered on this platform: Ram
  Raja Temple in Orchha (Madhya Pradesh), where Rama is worshipped
  uniquely as a reigning king; Kalaram Temple in Nashik (Maharashtra),
  within the Panchavati forest associated with the exile; Sita
  Ramachandraswamy Temple in Bhadrachalam (Telangana), the Godavari-basin
  "Dakshina Ayodhya"; the Kamadgiri/Ram Ghat cluster in Chitrakoot
  (Madhya Pradesh-Uttar Pradesh border), the most extensively documented
  exile-period site outside Ayodhya itself; and Janaki Mandir in
  Sitamarhi (Bihar), Sita's traditional birthplace. This list reflects an
  editorial judgement about which non-Ayodhya Rama sites are most
  significant nationally; it is not an exhaustive account of every
  regional Rama temple in India.
