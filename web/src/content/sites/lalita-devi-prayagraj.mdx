overview: |
  Lalita Devi Temple, in Meerapur, Prayagraj, close to the Triveni
  Sangam confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and legendary Sarasvati, is
  dedicated to Lalita Tripurasundari within the Srividya tradition of
  Shaktism. This platform notes a genuine naming overlap in composite
  Shakti Peetha source lists between this temple and the separately
  documented Alopi Devi Temple, also in Prayagraj, both associated with
  the same Sati's-fingers/right-hand attribution; this platform treats
  the two as distinct, physically separate temples rather than resolving
  which one is the "true" classical Shakti Peetha, consistent with the
  caveat already carried on this platform's Alopi Devi Temple page.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's structure is modelled directly on a Sri Yantra, the
  geometric diagram central to Srividya worship of Lalita Tripurasundari,
  and this Sri Yantra form is itself used in the temple's ritual
  practice, giving Lalita Devi Temple a distinctive architectural-
  devotional unity uncommon among the sites on this platform. Three
  aspects of the Divine Feminine, Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, and
  Mahasaraswati, are worshipped here in the form of three sacred pindis
  (aniconic stone representations), alongside the primary Lalita
  identity. Bathing at the nearby Sangam before visiting the temple is a
  widely observed devotional sequence among pilgrims.
