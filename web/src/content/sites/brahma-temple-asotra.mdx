overview: |
  Shri Kheteshwar Brahmadham Teerth, in Asotra village near Balotra in
  Rajasthan's Barmer district, is popularly described as the world's
  second dedicated Brahma temple, after the far older and more widely
  known Brahma Temple at Pushkar. Unlike Pushkar's temple, whose origins
  lie in ancient legend, Asotra's temple has a fully documented modern
  founding.

history: |
  The temple was founded by Brahmarshi Sant Khetaram Ji Maharaj with the
  explicit intention of establishing a further dedicated site for Brahma
  worship, given how few such temples exist elsewhere despite Brahma's
  status as a principal deity of the Hindu Trimurti. Its foundation stone
  was laid on 20 April 1961, and the main idol was formally consecrated
  on 6 May 1984, giving the temple a precisely documented modern
  history unusual among the sites covered on this platform.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Asotra's temple is understood devotionally as directly addressing the
  scarcity of dedicated Brahma worship sites in India, a scarcity itself
  traditionally explained by the Pushkar curse narrative (see this
  platform's Pushkar page). The temple's construction uses Jaisalmer's
  golden sandstone for its main entrance hall and Jodhpur (Chhitar)
  stone elsewhere, with a marble idol of Brahma, giving it a distinct
  regional Rajasthani architectural character.
