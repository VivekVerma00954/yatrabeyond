overview: |
  Doleshwar Mahadev Temple, in Suryavinayak, Bhaktapur, roughly 20 km from
  Kathmandu, is devotionally regarded as the "head" counterpart to
  Kedarnath in Uttarakhand, India — completing, in this framing, the shared
  bull-form Shiva legend that also underlies the Panch Kedar circuit
  (see the Panch Kedar and Kedarnath pages for the fuller account of that
  legend). Where Kedarnath's hump-shaped rock is worshipped as Shiva's
  body, Doleshwar is worshipped as his head.

  **An important note on the timeline:** this connection between Doleshwar
  and Kedarnath was formally and publicly declared only in 2009, by Shree
  1008 Jagat Guru Bheemashaankarling Shivacharya, then head priest of
  Kedarnath — not something documented continuously since ancient times.
  Nepali sources describe the underlying link as having existed in local
  tradition and temple ritual practice for much longer beforehand, but the
  2009 declaration is what brought the connection to wide public and
  pilgrim attention. Treat the "4,000 years" framing used in some tourism
  material as devotional rather than historically documented, pending
  expert verification.

history: |
  The shared legend traces to the same Mahabharata-era Pandava narrative
  underlying Kedarnath and the Panch Kedar: after the Kurukshetra war, the
  Pandavas sought Shiva's forgiveness for killing their own kin. Shiva,
  reluctant to grant an easy audience, took the form of a bull and fled
  into the Himalaya. When the Pandavas caught hold of the bull, they seized
  only its tail while its head separated from the body. Nepali tradition
  holds that this head resurfaced at what is now Doleshwar, while the body
  is venerated at Kedarnath in India.

  Since the 2009 declaration by Kedarnath's head priest formally
  identifying Doleshwar as this "head" site, temple ritual practice at
  Doleshwar — including its daily worship schedule and evening aarati —
  has been aligned with Kedarnath's own ritual conventions, reinforcing the
  devotional link between the two sites.

spiritualSignificance: |
  For pilgrims unable to make the demanding journey to Kedarnath itself,
  Doleshwar offers a devotionally significant, far more accessible
  alternative point of connection to the same Shiva legend, located within
  an easy day trip of Kathmandu. Since 2009, growing numbers of pilgrims,
  including from India, have visited Doleshwar specifically because of its
  declared link to Kedarnath, and some now treat a visit to both sites as
  a form of paired pilgrimage.

scriptures: |
  As with Kedarnath and the Panch Kedar, the underlying bull-form legend

  specific identification of Doleshwar as the "head" site is a matter of
  the 2009 declaration and subsequent devotional practice rather than a
