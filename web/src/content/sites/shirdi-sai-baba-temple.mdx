overview: |
  Shri Sai Baba Temple (Shree Samadhi Mandir) in Shirdi, Ahmednagar
  district, Maharashtra, is the samadhi (memorial shrine) of Sai Baba of
  Shirdi, a saint who lived and taught in the town through the late 19th
  and early 20th centuries and whose devotional following today spans
  Hindu, Muslim, and other communities. This platform notes Sai Baba's
  comparatively recent, well-documented historical life, distinct from
  the deep antiquity of most classical deities covered on this platform.

history: |
  Sai Baba is recorded as having arrived in Shirdi around 1858, initially
  living on the village outskirts and later under a neem tree at a spot
  now called Gurusthan, before settling in an abandoned mosque that
  became known as Dwarkamai. He died on 15 October 1918, coinciding with
  Vijayadashami, and was interred at Buti Wada in Shirdi, a site
  subsequently developed into the present Samadhi Mandir. Construction
  was initiated by Shreemant Gopalrao Buti, a wealthy devotee from
  Nagpur, who had originally envisioned the building as a residential
  mansion (wada) for Baba during his lifetime; significant expansion of
  the temple complex followed in the 1950s and 1960s.
