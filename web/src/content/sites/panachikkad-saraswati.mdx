overview: |
  Saraswathi Temple in Panachikkad, Kottayam district, Kerala, is one of
  the state's most significant dedicated Saraswati shrines and is
  popularly known as the "Mookambika of the South," drawing a comparison
  to the well-known Mookambika Devi Temple in Karnataka. Unlike many
  Saraswati temples, which conduct dedicated worship mainly during
  Navratri, Panachikkad performs continuous daily pooja to the goddess
  throughout the year.

history: |
  According to the temple's account, recorded in Kottarathil Sankunni's
  well-known 19th/early-20th century Malayalam work Aithihyamala, the
  site's history extends back over a thousand years and is traditionally
  linked to Parasurama, the legendary founder-figure of Kerala, who is
  said to have ploughed the site's originally rocky terrain to please
  Goddess Durga, who then blessed the location.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple's idol stands within a low-lying, naturally spring-fed area
  without an overhead roof, giving the impression of the goddess seated
  amid a small pond; the idol itself is largely covered by creepers and
  shrubs, whose leaves are devotionally regarded as sacred to Saraswati.
  The continuous flow of spring water at the goddess's feet, understood
  to never run dry even in peak summer, is the source of the site's very
  name — Saraswati, here understood through her association with
  flowing water (saras).
