overview: |
  Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple, in Tiruchanur (also called Alamelu
  Mangapuram), near Tirupati, is dedicated to Padmavathi, worshipped as
  an incarnation of Lakshmi and the consort of Venkateswara. Temple
  tradition holds that a pilgrimage to Tirumala's Venkateswara Temple is
  considered incomplete without first visiting Padmavathi here, giving
  Tiruchanur a devotional role directly linked to, but distinct from,
  Tirumala's own much larger pilgrimage.

history: |
  According to temple legend, Padmavathi (identified as an incarnation
  of Mahalakshmi) was born to Akasha Raja, a local ruler, and is said to
  have emerged from a lotus at the temple's sacred tank (Tiruchanur
  Pushkarini). She later married Venkateswara at the Kalyana Venkateswara
  Temple in nearby Narayanavanam, after which the couple moved to
  Tirumala, while Padmavathi is understood to continue residing at
  Tiruchanur/Alamelu Mangapuram. Within the wider temple complex, the
  Krishna Swamy Temple is dated to 1221 CE, making it the oldest
  identifiable structure on the site, while the Sundararaja Swamy Temple
  (dedicated to a form of Vishnu and his consorts) was added in the 16th
  century.
