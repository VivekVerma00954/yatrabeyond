overview: |
  Sri Naganathaswamy Temple in Thirunageswaram, on the outskirts of
  Kumbakonam, is the Rahu Navagraha Sthalam, dedicated to Shiva as
  Naganathar with consort Parvati as Piraisoodi Amman. The temple is
  distinctive among Rahu shrines for depicting the deity with a human
  face rather than the serpent face more typically seen elsewhere.

history: |
  The temple is believed to have been built by the Chola king Aditya
  Chola I in the 10th century CE, with later rulers making further
  additions, including four gateway towers (gopurams). The presiding
  deity is honoured in the 7th-century Tevaram, the canonical devotional
  work of the Tamil Nayanmar saints, giving the site Paadal Petra Sthalam
  status.

spiritualSignificance: |
  A widely cited devotional phenomenon at this temple is that milk
  poured over the image of Rahu during abhishekam (ritual bathing)
  reportedly turns blue, an occurrence regarded by devotees as a
  distinctive miracle specific to this site and a significant draw for
  pilgrims from considerable distances. The temple's ten-day
  Brahmotsavam, held during the Tamil month of Karthigai
  (November-December), is its most significant annual festival.
