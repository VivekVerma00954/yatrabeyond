overview: |
  **This page describes a site whose precise identity is disputed across
  sources; it is presented here clearly flagged, with all available
  information, so the full 18 Maha Shakti Peetha list can be shown on
  this platform.** Shankari Devi Shakti Peetha is a small shrine within
  the historic Koneswaram Temple complex on Swami Rock, Trincomalee, Sri
  Lanka, one of the Ashtadasha (18) Maha Shakti Peethas as described in
  the Ashta Dasa Shakti Peetha Stotram attributed to Adi Shankaracharya.
  Most traditional lists identify this as the site where Sati's yoni
  (groin) fell; a variant account instead links the site to her anklet
  (nupura). This platform presents both attributions rather than
  asserting one as settled. In local practice, the goddess is worshipped
  as Shankari and Shiva as Konesar (Trikoneshwara).

history: |
  The original shrine, along with much of the historic Koneswaram
  Temple complex, was destroyed by Portuguese colonial forces after
  1505 CE; temple materials were reportedly used in the construction of
  the nearby Fort Frederick. A new Shankari Devi shrine has since been
  established adjacent to the rebuilt Koneswaram Shiva Temple, though
  this is understood by available sources as a modern restoration
  rather than a continuously standing structure from before the
  Portuguese destruction. This platform found no source able to
  independently confirm the shrine's pre-1505 physical form or exact
  founding date, and presents this history with that limitation
  explicit rather than filling the gap with assumption.
