overview: |
  Vaishno Devi Temple, a cave shrine on the Trikuta Hills near Katra in
  Jammu and Kashmir's Reasi district, is one of the most visited pilgrimage
  sites in India, drawing many millions of pilgrims annually to a
  naturally formed cave shrine at approximately 1,500 m. The goddess
  Vaishno Devi is worshipped here as a combined manifestation of the three
  great goddesses of the Tridevi — Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, and Mahasarasvati
  — represented within the cave by three natural rock formations (pindis)
  rather than a sculpted image.

history: |
  According to widely circulated local tradition, the cave shrine was
  revealed around the 10th century CE to a Brahmin priest, Pandit
  Shridhar, of the nearby village of Hansali. In this account, the deity
  was originally venerated locally as "Trikuta Devi," after the
  three-peaked mountain on which the cave sits, before becoming more
  widely known as Vaishno Devi. The shrine's documented institutional
  history includes its inclusion in the Dharmarth Trust established in
  1846 by Maharaja Gulab Singh of Jammu and Kashmir, confirming its
  established status by the mid-19th century at the latest, whatever its
  earlier, less formally documented history may have been.

  A separate and widely told legend concerns Bhairon Nath, a disciple sent
  by the yogi Guru Gorakhnath to test whether the goddess had attained
  full spiritual mastery. Bhairon Nath is said to have instead fallen in
  pursuit of her, pressing her to marry him; fleeing to protect her
  meditation (tapasya), the goddess ultimately killed him in the cave now
  known as the shrine, and Bhairon Nath, realising his error as he died,
  asked for forgiveness. Devotional tradition holds that a Vaishno Devi
  pilgrimage is considered incomplete unless the pilgrim also visits the
  separate Bhairon Nath temple on the return journey.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Vaishno Devi's identity as a unified manifestation of the three Tridevi
  goddesses, rather than a single deity, gives the site a distinctive
  theological completeness within Shakta tradition — pilgrims receive
  darshan of Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, and Mahasarasvati together in a single
  visit. The pilgrimage route itself passes several sacred waypoints
  considered significant in their own right, including Ban Ganga (where
  pilgrims traditionally cleanse themselves early in the trek), Ardhkuwari
  (a cave roughly midway, associated with the goddess's own meditation
  during her flight from Bhairon Nath), Charan Paduka, and Sanjichhat.

scriptures: |

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** The shrine is open year-round, unlike most Himalayan
  pilgrimage sites on this platform. March–May and September–November
  offer the most comfortable trekking weather; the Navratri festivals
  (spring and autumn) draw especially large crowds, given Vaishno Devi's
  status as a major Shakti pilgrimage.

  **Getting there:** Katra, the base town, is reached by road from Jammu
  (roughly 50 km), which has both an airport and a major railway station;
  Katra also has its own railway station with direct connections to
  several major Indian cities.

  **The trek:** Approximately 13–14 km from Katra to the main shrine
  (Bhawan), on a well-maintained, well-lit path with rest points, food
  stalls, and medical aid posts throughout. Pony, palanquin, battery car
  (for part of the route), and helicopter services (Katra to Sanjichhat,
  reducing the walk to a short final stretch) are all available and
  commonly used, particularly by older pilgrims or those with limited
  mobility.

  **Registration:** Pilgrims must obtain a Yatra Registration Slip, free
  of charge, from counters in Katra or online via the Shri Mata Vaishno
  Devi Shrine Board, before beginning the trek.

preservation: |
  Given the very high visitor volume — among the highest of any pilgrimage
  site on this platform — the Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board manages
  substantial infrastructure investment in trail maintenance, waste
  management, and crowd control, making this one of the better-resourced
