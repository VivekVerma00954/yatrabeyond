overview: |
  Prem Mandir ("Temple of Divine Love"), on the outskirts of Vrindavan,
  is a large, entirely modern temple complex dedicated to Radha-Krishna
  and Sita-Rama, built by Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj. Constructed
  over roughly 12 years and inaugurated in 2012, the temple is built
  almost entirely of Italian marble and has become one of Braj's most
  visited sites, particularly known for its elaborate evening
  illumination and fountain displays.

history: |
  The temple's origin traces to a personal vow made by Shri Kripalu
  Maharaj in 1946, at the age of 24, to one day build a grand temple in
  Vrindavan dedicated to divine love. The foundation stone was laid on 14
  January 2001 before thousands of devotees, and construction, involving
  around 1,000 artisans working with Italian marble, took approximately
  12 years and an estimated cost of around ₹150 crore. The completed
  temple was inaugurated across a multi-day ceremony from 15 to 17
  February 2012.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Prem Mandir's scale and modern construction make it a distinctive
  counterpoint to Braj's older, more historically layered temples —
  representing a specifically 21st-century expression of Vaishnava
  devotion, built with contemporary resources and design sensibilities
  while housing traditional deity forms. Its detailed marble carvings
  depict scenes from Krishna's and Rama's lives throughout the complex,
  and its evening light-and-water shows have made it a significant
  devotional and visual experience for a very broad range of visitors,
  including many who might not otherwise visit a traditional temple.

scriptures: |
  Prem Mandir's central theological focus on divine love (prem) between
  Radha and Krishna, and Sita and Rama, draws on Gaudiya and broader
  Vaishnava devotional literature and the teachings of Jagadguru Kripalu
  Maharaj himself, rather than being tied to a single ancient founding
  legend, as with several of Braj's older temples.
