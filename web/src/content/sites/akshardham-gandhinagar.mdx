overview: |
  Swaminarayan Akshardham in Gandhinagar, Gujarat, is the original
  Akshardham temple complex, opened to the public in 1992 as a tribute
  to Bhagwan Swaminarayan and his life and teachings. It served as the
  design and construction precedent for the later, larger Akshardham
  complex in Delhi, with much of the team behind the Delhi project having
  gained direct experience working on this earlier Gandhinagar complex.

history: |
  The complex was inspired by Yogiji Maharaj (1892-1971), the fourth
  spiritual successor within the BAPS lineage of the Swaminarayan
  tradition, and was created under the guidance of Pramukh Swami Maharaj
  (1921-2016), his fifth successor. Construction took roughly 13 years,
  culminating in the complex's opening in 1992.

spiritualSignificance: |
  As with its later Delhi counterpart, the Gandhinagar complex is
  dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan (1781-1830), founder of the
  Swaminarayan Sampraday, a Vaishnava-tradition devotional and reform
  movement originating in Gujarat. The central shrine forms the focal
  point of the wider complex, with surrounding gardens and exhibition
  halls giving the site a combined devotional and cultural-educational
  character, a template later expanded upon at Akshardham Delhi.
