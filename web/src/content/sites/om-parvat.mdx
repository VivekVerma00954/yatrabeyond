overview: |
  Om Parvat is a Himalayan peak in Pithoragarh district, Uttarakhand,
  near the India-Nepal-Tibet tri-junction, distinctive for the natural
  pattern formed by snow lodged in the rock crevices of its face, which
  devotees and visitors read as the sacred syllable "Om." It sits close
  to, and is commonly visited together with, Adi Kailash, viewed from
  the same general border-region pilgrimage route via Pithoragarh,
  Dharchula, and Gunji.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** May to mid-October is the practical window, with
  June and September widely cited as the most reliable months for clear
  weather and good visibility of the Om formation; the route is closed
  by snow outside this window, and the Om pattern itself may be less
  visible depending on current snow cover.

  **Getting there:** The journey begins at Pithoragarh and proceeds via
  Dharchula and Gunji to the Nabhidhang viewpoint, largely on motorable
  border-area roads, with only a short final walk on uneven ground. An
  Inner Line Permit is required, as the route runs through a restricted
  border zone; this is typically arranged through registered tour
  operators based in Pithoragarh or Dharchula.
