overview: |
  Kanak Bhawan ("Golden Palace"), in Ayodhya's Ramkot area northeast of
  the main citadel, is a temple whose name and legend both centre on a
  personal gift: tradition holds that Queen Kaikeyi, Rama's stepmother,
  presented this palace to Sita as a wedding gift when the newly married
  couple arrived in Ayodhya, intended for Sita's own exclusive use. The
  present temple, in Bundela architectural style, houses three ornately
  crowned pairs of Rama and Sita idols within its sanctum.

history: |
  While the site's origins are described in ancient devotional tradition,
  the present structure's more recent, better-documented history dates
  the temple's substantial reconstruction to 1891, funded and overseen by
  Vrish Bhanu Kunwari. Some sources also link the temple's rebuilding
  history to the wider Vikramaditya-era tradition of temple construction
  and restoration across Ayodhya, referenced at several of the city's
  ancient sites, though as with those other sites, this attribution
  should be treated as devotional and regional historical tradition
  rather than independently verified archaeological fact.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Kanak Bhawan's central devotional focus on Sita, alongside Rama, gives
  it a distinctive place within Ayodhya's temple cluster, most of which
  centre more directly on Rama or Hanuman individually. The temple is
  particularly associated with devotion to the ideal of marital devotion
  and domestic harmony represented by Rama and Sita's relationship within
  Vaishnava tradition.
