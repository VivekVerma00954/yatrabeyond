overview: |
  The Chauth Mata Temple, at Chauth Ka Barwara in Sawai Madhopur
  district, Rajasthan, is widely cited as the oldest and most prominent
  temple dedicated to Chauth Mata (also called Karva Mata), the folk
  goddess most closely associated with the Karva Chauth fasting
  tradition observed by married Hindu women across North India.

history: |
  The temple is recorded as having been built in 1451 by King Bhim
  Singh. It sits roughly 1,100 feet above the surrounding town on
  Shaktigiri hill, built in white marble in a traditional Rajput
  architectural style, with an adjoining shrine housing images of
  Ganesha and Bhairav alongside the main sanctum.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Chauth Mata (Karva Mata) is worshipped as a manifestation of Goddess
  Parvati. Karva Chauth, the temple's associated annual festival, is
  observed by married Hindu women who fast from sunrise until moonrise,
  praying for the health and long life of their husbands; a related
  observance, Til Chauth, is also marked at the same site. Large fairs
  are organised at the temple during both festivals, drawing substantial
  numbers of devotees.
