history: |
  The exact founding date of the temple is not clearly established, but
  the present structure is generally dated to the mid-17th century,
  reportedly built by the Maratha army commander Sardar Vinchurkar in the
  Nagara style of temple architecture. Kaal Bhairav's significance at
  Kashi is referenced in the Skanda Purana, giving the site's devotional
  tradition a considerably older textual basis than its standing
  structure.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Kaal Bhairav is understood in Kashi tradition as holding ultimate
  administrative and protective authority over the city: no one is
  traditionally believed able to reside in or enter Varanasi without his
  sanction, and pilgrims conventionally seek his blessing before
  beginning any wider spiritual undertaking in Kashi. This "Kotwal" role
  is treated with striking literalness locally: the temple's presiding
  officer is said to occupy the senior chair at the local police station,
  with incoming officials, including IAS and IPS officers posted to the
  city, customarily worshipping Kaal Bhairav before beginning their
  duties. The temple's silver-faced idol, seated on his vahana (a dog)
  and holding a trident, has only its garlanded face visible to visitors
  through the sanctum doorway, with the rest of the image kept covered
  by cloth.
