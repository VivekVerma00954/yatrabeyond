overview: |
  Saptashrungi Temple, on a hilltop in Vani, roughly 60 km from Nashik,
  Maharashtra, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas and one of
  Maharashtra's own "three and a half Shakti Peethas," alongside
  Mahalakshmi Kolhapur, Tuljapur, and Mahur, the first of which is
  covered elsewhere on this platform. Sources give differing body-part
  attributions (the chin, in two parts, or the right arm); this platform
  notes both rather than asserting one as settled. The presiding
  goddess, Bhramari, is depicted with 18 arms and stands roughly 10 feet
  tall.

history: |
  According to legend, the sage Markandeya (composer of the Devi
  Mahatmyam) and the sage Parashar performed intense penance at
  Saptashrungi, and when the goddess appeared to aid the deities against
  a demon threat, a swarm of black bees emerged from her body and
  destroyed the attackers, giving the presiding goddess her name
  Bhramari (from bhramar, "bee").

spiritualSignificance: |
  Saptashrungi's identity as one of Maharashtra's own "three and a half
  Shakti Peethas," a distinct regional devotional framework separate
  from, though overlapping with, the classical 51 Shakti Peethas, gives
  it a dual significance similar to Mahalakshmi Kolhapur's own dual
  classification, also covered on this platform.

scriptures: |
