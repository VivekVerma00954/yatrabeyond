overview: |
  Amarkantak, in Anuppur district, Madhya Pradesh, is unique among this
  platform's Shakti Peethas in hosting two separate, closely situated
  sites, both counted among the classical 51: **Narmada Udgam**, at the
  source of the sacred Narmada river, where Sati's right buttock is said
  to have fallen, with the goddess worshipped as Narmada Devi and Shiva
  as Bhadrasen; and **Kalmadhav**, a short distance away, where Sati's
  left buttock is said to have fallen, with the goddess worshipped as
  Kali or Kalmadhav Devi. Sources describe this dual presence as a
  phenomenon believed to be unique among the 51 sites.

history: |
  Both temples are often dated to the 10th-11th century, with
  construction flourishing under the Kalachuri dynasty; some accounts
  place Kalmadhav's construction as late as the 12th century under the
  same dynasty. The Narmada Udgam Kund (the pool marking the river's
  birthplace) was built by Rewa Nayak rulers, with the Bhonsle rulers of
  Nagpur later shaping the Narmada Mandir, and Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar,
  whose temple patronage is referenced at several other sites across
  this platform, carrying out a further renovation.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The pairing of Narmada Udgam and Kalmadhav is understood in regional
  devotional tradition as a complete, twofold manifestation of the
  Divine Mother's full essence at the single geographic heart of the
  Maikal range, distinct from the platform's other Shakti Peethas, which
  each represent a single, standalone body-part attribution.
