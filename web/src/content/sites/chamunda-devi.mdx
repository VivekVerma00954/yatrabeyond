overview: |
  Chamunda Devi Temple, also called Chamunda Nandikeshwar Dham, in
  Dharamshala Tehsil, Kangra district, is a major Himachal Pradesh Devi
  temple and Siddha Peetha (a site of powerful spiritual attainment).
  **Importantly, and unlike the other four temples in this platform's
  Himachal cluster, Chamunda Devi is not listed among the classical 51 or
  52 Shakti Peethas** named in texts such as the Kalika Purana and Devi
  Bhagavata Purana. It is, however, deeply revered regionally and
  commonly grouped alongside Chintpurni, Jwala Ji, Brajeshwari, and Naina
  Devi as one of Himachal Pradesh's five major Shakti temples in popular
  and tourism usage — a grouping this platform preserves for practical
  navigation purposes while being explicit about the underlying
  classification difference.

history: |
  The present, more accessible temple is generally dated to the 16th
  century, with some sources specifying 1762 CE and crediting Raja Umed
  Singh of Chamba with its establishment. It was built specifically to
  provide easier access for pilgrims, given that the older, original
  shrine — Aadi Himani Chamunda — sits at a higher, harder-to-reach
  hilltop location. Temple legend describes a king and priest seeking the
  goddess's blessing to relocate her worship to a more accessible site;
  the goddess is said to have appeared in the priest's dream, revealing
  the location of an ancient idol, which was then brought to the present
  temple.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Chamunda's core mythology describes the goddess Kali defeating the
  demons Chanda and Munda in a fierce battle at this location, with her
  name directly combining theirs. This gives the site a devotional
  identity centred on the destruction of specific named demonic forces,
  distinct from the Sati-dismemberment narrative underlying the
  classical Shakti Peethas nearby. As a Siddha Peetha, the site is
  particularly associated by devotees with divine intervention and the
  fulfilment of difficult personal petitions.

scriptures: |
  The Chanda-Munda legend is documented in the Devi Mahatmya (Chandi
  Path) section of the Markandeya Purana. Chamunda Devi's exclusion from
  the classical 51/52 Shakti Peetha lists, despite her regional
  prominence, reflects a genuine textual distinction rather than an
  oversight, and should be presented to readers accurately.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable
  weather.

  **Getting there:** Located roughly 19 km from Palampur town in
  Dharamshala Tehsil, reached by road; Dharamshala's Gaggal Airport is
  the nearest air connection.

  **The older shrine:** Aadi Himani Chamunda, the original hilltop
  shrine, is a separate, more demanding pilgrimage than the main,
  accessible temple, and should be treated as an optional extension
  rather than assumed to be part of a standard visit.
