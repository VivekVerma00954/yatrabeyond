overview: |
  The Mallikarjuna Temple at Srisailam, in Andhra Pradesh's Nandyal
  district, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Shiva and, unusually,
  simultaneously one of the 52 Shakti Peethas associated with the goddess
  — making it sacred to both Shaivite and Shakta traditions at the same
  site. Shiva is worshipped here as Mallikarjuna and Parvati as
  Bhramaramba. The temple stands on the Nallamala hills above the Krishna
  River.

history: |
  Tradition holds that Shiva and Parvati travelled to Srisailam to comfort
  their son Kartikeya, who had left Mount Kailash upset after Ganesha's
  marriage. Shiva is said to have manifested here as Mallikarjuna and
  Parvati as Bhramaramba so as to remain near their son without directly
  overriding his wish for solitude. A further tradition holds that Shiva
  appears here as "Arjuna" on the new moon (Amavasya) and Parvati as
  "Mallika" on the full moon (Poornima), giving the site its combined name.

  The temple received patronage across many centuries and dynasties,
  including the Satavahanas (2nd century CE), the Chalukyas, the
  Kakatiyas, and the Vijayanagara emperors, with the core shrine dated to
  around the 7th century and substantial expansion under later rulers.
  The complex's fortress-like outer walls and four gopurams (temple
  towers) reflect this long history of successive royal patronage and
  expansion.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Srisailam's dual status as both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha gives
  it a distinctive place in Hindu pilgrimage — a rare site where the
  Shaivite and Shakta traditions converge in a single, continuously active
  temple complex. The site also contains a Sahasra Linga (a shrine with a
  thousand small lingams), traditionally attributed to Rama, and five
  further lingams traditionally attributed to the Pandavas, adding further
  devotional layers to the complex.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to February offers the most comfortable
  weather for the forest drive and temple visit; summer (March–June) can
  be very hot, and the monsoon brings heavy rain to the Nallamala forest
  region.

  **Getting there:** Srisailam is reached by road from Hyderabad
  (approximately 210 km) or Kurnool, via winding ghat roads through the
  Nallamala forest and the Srisailam Tiger Reserve. The nearest railway
  stations with fuller connectivity are at Kurnool or Markapur Road, with
  onward road travel required.
