overview: |
  The Vishwakarma Temple near Kamakhya Gate, at the base of Nilachal Hill
  in Guwahati, Assam, is popularly described in regional reporting as the
  oldest dedicated temple to Vishwakarma, the divine architect and
  craftsman deity of Hindu tradition, in India. Dedicated temples to
  Vishwakarma are considerably rarer than those for most major deities
  covered on this platform, making Guwahati's temple a distinctive
  destination for artisans, engineers, and craftspeople specifically.

history: |
  According to locally reported accounts, the temple was founded by a
  priest associated with the nearby Kamakhya Temple and constructed by
  Mahabir Prasad Dhirasaria, with land for the site donated by Pandit
  Bhabakanta Sharma; most sources place its establishment around 1959-60,
  with the temple formally dated to 1965. Its location at the base of
  Nilachal Hill situates it within the same wider sacred landscape as
  Kamakhya Temple, one of the most significant Shakti Peethas in India,
  though the two are entirely distinct in deity and devotional purpose.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Vishwakarma is venerated in Hindu tradition as the architect of the
  universe and the divine craftsman credited with building the palaces
  and weapons of the gods; artisans, engineers, and craftspeople
  particularly revere him as the patron deity of their work. Vishwakarma
  Puja, observed especially in West Bengal, Assam, Odisha, Tripura,
  Bihar, and Jharkhand, is the temple's principal annual festival,
  drawing devotees connected to trades, industry, and craftsmanship
  specifically, distinguishing this temple's devotional community from
  the broader pilgrimage base seen at most other sites on this platform.
