overview: |
  Vishwanath Temple, on the banks of the Bhagirathi river in Uttarkashi,
  Uttarakhand, is regarded in local tradition as one of the oldest
  temples in the town and is treated as a devotional counterpart to
  Kashi Vishwanath in Varanasi, offering pilgrims unable to travel to
  Varanasi a comparable site closer to the Himalaya. The temple is a key
  stop on the Chota Char Dham route, alongside Yamunotri, Gangotri,
  Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

history: |
  Accounts of the temple's founding vary across sources: one tradition
  credits King Ganeshwar and his son Guh, described as a great warrior
  who built the temple's trident, while another tradition credits
  Parashurama, an incarnation of Vishnu and a disciple of Shiva, with
  installing a 56 cm Shivalinga tilted towards the south. The temple was
  renovated in 1857 CE by Maharani Khaneti, queen of Tehri and wife of
  Sudarshan Shah. These varying foundation accounts should be
  reconciled and verified by subject-matter editors before publication.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple houses a sacred Shivalinga worshipped as Vishwanath, "Lord
  of the Universe," directly echoing the naming and devotional
  character of Kashi Vishwanath in Varanasi; in Hindu tradition, Kashi
  is understood as a place where moksha (liberation) may be attained,
  and this Uttarkashi temple is regarded by many pilgrims as a
  Himalayan counterpart for those unable to travel to Varanasi itself.
