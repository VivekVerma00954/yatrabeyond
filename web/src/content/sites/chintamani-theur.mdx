overview: |
  Chintamani Temple in Theur, roughly 25 km from Pune, is one of the
  larger and more famous of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines, closely
  associated historically with the Peshwa rulers of Maharashtra,
  particularly Madhavrao I, who both renovated the temple and spent his
  final days within its precincts.

history: |
  Though believed to have existed since antiquity, the current structure
  is credited to the Ganapatya saint Morya Gosavi (whose dates are
  variously placed between the 13th and 17th centuries) or his
  descendants. The temple was subsequently renovated and expanded by
  Peshwa Madhavrao I (reigned 1745-1772), who is said to have visited
  both before and after military engagements to seek Ganesha's blessing
  and give thanks, and who ultimately spent his last days at the temple.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The temple takes its name from the Chintamani, a mythical
  wish-fulfilling gem which, according to legend, Ganesha recovered from
  a greedy demon named Guna and returned to the sage Kapila, giving the
  deity his name here as the bestower of the gem's wish-granting power.
  Beyond the main Ganesha shrine, the temple complex includes several
  smaller shrines (to Shiva, to Vishnu-Lakshmi, and to Hanuman), giving
  Theur a broader devotional complex than some of the other Ashtavinayak
  sites.
