overview: |
  Naganathaswamy Temple in Keezhaperumpallam (also called Vanagiri), near
  Thiruvenkadu in Mayiladuthurai district, is the Ketu Navagraha Sthalam,
  and among the most visited dedicated Ketu temples in South India. The
  main deity, Naganatha Swamy, is worshipped alongside consort
  Soundaryanaki, with Ketu specifically enshrined facing west within the
  temple's north praharam (precinct).

history: |
  The temple is credited to construction under the Chola kings, giving
  it a mediaeval royal patronage history in keeping with several other
  Navagraha Sthalams in the same wider Cauvery Delta region. It features
  a two-tier rajagopuram surrounded by two prakarams.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Ketu's iconography here, depicted with a serpent's head and an asura's
  (demon's) body, directly reflects the Puranic legend of the Samudra
  Manthan (churning of the cosmic ocean): when the asura Rahu disguised
  himself to drink the nectar of immortality (amrita) meant only for the
  gods, Vishnu (in his Mohini form) alerted Surya and Chandra, and Vishnu
  severed Rahu's head in anger; because the nectar had already been
  consumed, both the head (Rahu) and the body (which became Ketu)
  continued to live on independently. Ketu is devotionally understood to
  bring prosperity, and to remove the effects of snakebite and
  poison-related illness.
