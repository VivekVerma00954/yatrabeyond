overview: |
  Nangli Sahib, also called Nangli Tirath or Nangli Dham, in Nangli
  village near Sakoti Tanda, Meerut district, Uttar Pradesh, is the
  samadhi (memorial shrine) of Satguru Swami Swarupanand Ji Maharaj
  (1884-1936), a guru of the Paramhans Advait Mat lineage, popularly
  revered here as "Nangli Niwasi Bhagwan" and "Hari Har Baba." **This
  platform is explicit that Nangli Sahib is not a classical Puranic
  deity temple** in the way most sites on this platform are; it is a
  guru-samadhi and ashram complex within the wider Sant Mat/Advait
  (non-dualist) devotional tradition, a category distinct from
  Vaishnavism, Shaivism, and Shaktism, though sharing devotional and
  pilgrimage characteristics with all three.

history: |
  Swami Swarupanand Ji was born Beli Ram on 1 February 1884 in Teri,
  Kohat district (in present-day Pakistan), into a Vasudeva family. He
  was initiated into sannyasa in the early 1900s by Paramhans Swami
  Advaitanand Ji, who gave him the name Swarupanand. During his
  teacher's lifetime and after, Swarupanand Ji established an order of
  renunciate monks across northern India and founded numerous ashrams to
  disseminate his guru's teachings. He died on 9 April 1936 in Nangli
  village, by which time he had, according to available sources, more
  than 10,000 followers and over 300 affiliated ashrams across northern
  India. His samadhi at Nangli subsequently became the central
  pilgrimage site of the tradition he founded, drawing devotees from
  across India.

spiritualSignificance: |
  One of Swarupanand Ji's own disciples, Hans Ji Maharaj, went on to
  found the Divine Light Mission, a further Sant Mat-lineage movement
  with its own substantial 20th-century following, giving Nangli Sahib
  an documented place within a wider genealogy of modern North Indian
  guru traditions extending well beyond the site itself. The approach
  road from the main highway to the shrine traditionally has 84 turns,
  symbolically representing liberation (mukti) from the cycle of the
  84 lakh yonis (the traditional count of life-forms through which a
  soul is believed to transmigrate before liberation), a devotional
  detail specific to this site.
