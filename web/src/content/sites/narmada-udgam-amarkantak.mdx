history: |
  This platform's `narmada-udgam-kalmadhav` page details the specific
  history of the Amarkantak source temple itself (Kalachuri-era origins,
  and renovation under Ahilyabai Holkar). Downstream at Maheshwar,
  Ahilyabai Holkar's 18th-century patronage as queen of the Holkar
  dynasty transformed the riverfront into a major cultural centre,
  commissioning numerous temples and a series of stone-paved ghats
  (roughly 28 in total, the most significant being Ahilya, Peshwa,
  Phanse, and Mahila ghats) along nearly 2 km of riverfront.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Narmada is traditionally called Shankari, "daughter of Shankar
  (Shiva)," with legend holding she was born from a drop of Shiva's
  tears, or in an alternate telling, from a drop of his sweat while
  dancing on the hills. The Narmada Parikrama, a complete
  circumambulation of the river along both banks traditionally taking
  many months to complete on foot, is considered one of the most
  significant and demanding pilgrimages associated with any Indian
  river, reflecting the exceptional sanctity attributed to the Narmada
  across the entire length of its course, not only at its source.
  Maheshwar's ghats, in particular, are where the river is most widely
  and visibly venerated by ordinary pilgrims on a daily basis.
