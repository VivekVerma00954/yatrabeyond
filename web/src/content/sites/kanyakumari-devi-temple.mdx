overview: |
  The Bhagavathi Amman Temple at Kanyakumari, at the southernmost tip of
  the Indian mainland where the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian
  Ocean meet, is one of the classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally
  marking the spot where Sati's back and spine fell. The presiding
  goddess, worshipped here as the eternal virgin (Kanyakumari), gives the
  entire town and district its name.

  narrative that gives the site its classical-51 classification.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Kanyakumari's location at the literal meeting point of three bodies of
  water gives this Shakti Peetha an unusually strong association with
  geographic finality and threshold, reflected in its role as one of the
  most visited pilgrimage and sunrise/sunset viewing destinations in
  South India, drawing both devotional and general tourism in roughly
  equal measure.
