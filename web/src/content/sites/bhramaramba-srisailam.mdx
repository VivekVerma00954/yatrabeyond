overview: |
  Bhramaramba Devi Temple, within the Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple
  complex at Srisailam on the Nallamala Hills, Andhra Pradesh, is one of
  the Ashtadasha (18) Maha Shakti Peethas, marking the spot where Sati's
  neck fell (kirtankar's composite list instead cites her right anklet,
  a discrepancy this platform flags rather than resolves). Srisailam is
  uniquely significant within this platform's coverage as one of the
  very few sites that is simultaneously one of the 12 Jyotirlingas
  (Mallikarjuna) and a major Shakti Peetha (Bhramaramba) at the same
  physical location.

history: |
  Srisailam became the site of a temple as early as the 2nd century CE,
  making it one of the more anciently dated temple sites referenced on
  this platform, with substantial later expansion and patronage across
  successive South Indian dynasties.

spiritualSignificance: |
  As a combined Jyotirlinga-and-Shakti-Peetha site, Srisailam represents
  a particularly complete expression of Shiva-Shakti union within a
  single temple complex, distinct from the more common pattern elsewhere
  on this platform of separate, nearby-but-distinct Shiva and Devi
  shrines.

scriptures: |
  Bhramaramba's identification within the Ashtadasha Maha Shakti Peetha
  tradition is documented in the Ashta Dasa Shakti Peetha Stotram
  attributed to Adi Shankaracharya. Specific verse-level citation
  requires expert verification before publication.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to February offers the most comfortable
  weather; Maha Shivaratri is the temple's single largest festival.

  **Getting there:** Srisailam is reached by road from Hyderabad
  (roughly 210 km) or Kurnool; there is no direct rail access to
  Srisailam itself.
