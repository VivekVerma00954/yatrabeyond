overview: |
  Mahaganapati Temple in Ranjangaon, Shirur taluka, Pune district, is one
  of the eight Ashtavinayak shrines and, by most sources, the temple
  traditionally visited last on the complete Ashtavinayak Yatra. Its
  deity, Mahaganapati, is regarded as the most powerful of the eight
  Ashtavinayak forms of Ganesha.

history: |
  The site's origins are generally dated to between the 9th and 10th
  centuries CE, with the main temple structure appearing to have been
  built or substantially developed during the Peshwa era. The temple's
  swayambhu idol is enclosed within a stone sanctum said to have been
  constructed by the Peshwa ruler Madhavrao, who, like at Theur, would
  halt here for darshan on his way to and from military campaigns. The
  visible Ganesha idol gifted to the temple is credited to the "Khollam"
  family, goldsmiths from Ranjangaon.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Mahaganapati ("the great Ganapati") is devotionally regarded as the
  most powerful of the eight Ashtavinayak forms, giving Ranjangaon
  particular weight as the traditional final stop of the full circuit,
  where pilgrims complete the yatra having sequentially sought each of
  Ganesha's distinct forms and their associated blessings.
