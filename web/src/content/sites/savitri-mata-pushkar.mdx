overview: |
  Savitri Mata Temple, atop Ratnagiri Hill in Pushkar, Rajasthan, is
  dedicated to Savitri, Brahma's principal consort, and is directly tied
  to the same founding legend as this platform's Brahma Temple and
  Gayatri Mata Temple pages: Savitri's anger at being displaced from
  Brahma's yagna by a second consort, Gayatri, which in tradition also
  gave rise to the broader curse limiting Brahma's worship largely to
  Pushkar alone.

history: |
  The temple's construction is credited to the Bangar family in the
  early 20th century, though it is said to house idols of Savitri and
  Gayatri dating to as early as the 7th century CE, considerably
  predating the visible structure. A ropeway to the temple, covering
  roughly 720 metres, was inaugurated in 2017 by Damodar Ropeways &
  Infra Limited, substantially easing access to what remains a
  physically demanding hilltop site.

spiritualSignificance: |
  According to the Pushkar legend, Savitri, arriving late for Brahma's
  yagna and finding Gayatri installed beside him as a substitute
  consort, cursed Brahma that he would never be worshipped anywhere in
  the world except at Pushkar itself — the very legend underlying this
  platform's Brahma Temple page and its description of Pushkar as the
  singular site of dedicated Brahma worship. Savitri's own temple, set
  higher and more physically demanding to reach than Gayatri's
  neighbouring hill shrine, is understood within local tradition as
  reflecting her continued primacy as Brahma's principal consort despite
  the yagna incident.
