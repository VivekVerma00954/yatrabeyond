overview: |
  Chintpurni Temple, in Una district, Himachal Pradesh, is one of the
  classical 51 Shakti Peethas, traditionally marking the spot where
  Sati's feet fell. The temple is closely associated with the Mahavidya
  Chhinnamastika ("the headless one"), depicted as a self-decapitated
  goddess in a Pindi (aniconic) form — one of the more visually and
  theologically distinctive goddess forms honoured at any Shakti Peetha
  on this platform.

history: |
  Local tradition credits Pandit Mai Das, a Punjabi Brahmin from the
  former Patiala princely state, with establishing this shrine at
  Chhaproh village approximately twelve generations ago; his descendants
  continue to serve as the temple's hereditary priests today. The temple's
  founding is understood devotionally as the identification of an
  already-sacred site rather than a fixed historical construction date,
  consistent with how several other Shakti Peethas on this platform
  describe their own origins.

spiritualSignificance: |
  The Chhinnamastika legend holds that the goddess severed her own head
  to satisfy the blood-thirst of her companions during a moment of
  spiritual crisis, an act of extreme self-sacrifice distinct from the
  more common goddess narratives found elsewhere in this platform's
  Shakti content. Puranic tradition associated with the site further
  holds that Chhinnamastika is protected in the four cardinal directions
  by four Shiva temples — Kaleshwar Mahadev (east), Narayana Mahadev
  (west), Muchkund Mahadev (north), and Shiva Bari (south) — each roughly
  equidistant from Chintpurni, giving the site a distinctive protective
  cosmological geography.

scriptures: |
  Chintpurni's identification with Sati's feet draws on Shakti Peetha
