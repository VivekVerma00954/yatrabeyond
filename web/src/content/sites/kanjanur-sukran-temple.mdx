overview: |
  Agniswarar Temple in Kanjanur, near Kumbakonam, is the sixth of the
  Navagraha Sthalams, dedicated to Shiva as Agniswarar, worshipped
  alongside Parvati as Karpavalli Amman, with Sukra (Venus) understood
  in temple tradition to reside within the body of the Shiva idol
  itself.

history: |
  The temple is attributed to the Medieval Cholas, with later renovation
  under the Vijayanagara Empire. It features a five-tier rajagopuram
  (entrance tower) surrounded by two prakarams (enclosed precincts).

spiritualSignificance: |
  Reflecting the wider Saivite understanding of Shiva as all-pervading,

  this temple among the nine Navagraha Sthalams. The temple is
  recognised as the Parihara Sthalam (remedy site) for Shukra Dosha, with
  devotees praying here for marriage harmony, financial stability,
  artistic talent, and relief from Venus-related astrological
  afflictions.
