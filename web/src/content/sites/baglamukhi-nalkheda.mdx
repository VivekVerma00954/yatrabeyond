overview: |
  Baglamukhi Temple, in Nalkheda, Madhya Pradesh, on the banks of the
  Lakhundar River (a tributary of the Narmada), is dedicated to
  Baglamukhi, the eighth of the ten Mahavidyas (Tantric wisdom goddesses).
  **This platform is explicit that Baglamukhi Nalkheda is not one of the
  classical 51 or 52 Shakti Peethas** — it belongs to a related but
  textually distinct devotional category, the Dasa Mahavidya tradition,
  and is not associated with the Sati-dismemberment legend underlying the
  Shakti Peethas covered elsewhere on this platform. It is included here
  as a major, widely visited Tantric pilgrimage site in its own right,
  not as a Shakti Peetha.

history: |
  According to temple tradition, the site's origins trace to the Dwapara
  Yuga of the Mahabharata era, when Yudhishthira, acting on Krishna's
  instruction, is said to have performed penance here to invoke divine
  aid for the Pandavas ahead of the Kurukshetra war — an origin legend
  that, notably, ties Nalkheda to the same broader Pandava-era
  devotional framework referenced at several other sites on this
  platform, including parts of the Panch Kedar and Ayodhya clusters. The
  present temple structure was restored in 1815, reflecting a more
  recent, better-documented phase of construction layered atop this much
  older devotional tradition.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Baglamukhi is a fierce manifestation of Shakti, traditionally invoked
  by devotees, sadhaks, and Tantric practitioners specifically in matters
  of legal disputes, conflict with adversaries, and professional or
  personal success — a markedly practical, outcome-oriented devotional
  focus distinct from the broader, more general protective and maternal
  themes found at most of the Shakti Peethas covered elsewhere on this
  platform. The temple's central icon is a distinctive triad, with
  Parvati at the centre in her Baglamukhi form, flanked by Lakshmi and
  Saraswati.
