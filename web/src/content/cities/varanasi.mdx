overview: |
  Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, sits on the western bank of
  the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh and is among the oldest continuously
  inhabited cities in the world, with roots reaching back roughly 3,000
  years. Revered as Shiva's own city, it is considered the holiest of
  the seven sacred cities of Hinduism and draws over a million pilgrims
  a year, alongside domestic and international visitors, to its more
  than 80 riverfront ghats and its dense concentration of ancient and
  rebuilt temples.

history: |
  Varanasi's name is popularly derived from the Varuna and Asi rivers,
  which join the Ganges near the city. It formed part of a kingdom as
  far back as roughly 2,500 years, and by the 8th century CE, Adi
  Shankaracharya had established formal Shiva worship as the city's
  central devotional practice, cementing its identity as Shiva's city.
  The city's major temples, including Kashi Vishwanath, have been
  repeatedly demolished and rebuilt across centuries of shifting
  political control; the present Kashi Vishwanath structure dates to
  1780, funded by Ahilyabai Holkar, following the site's most
  significant destruction under Aurangzeb in 1669. Varanasi has also
  long been a centre of learning, arts, and the Bhakti movement:
  Tulsidas composed the Ramcharitmanas here, and reformist poet-saints
  Kabir and Ravidas were born in the city.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Hindu tradition holds that dying in Varanasi and having one's ashes
  scattered in the Ganges grants moksha, liberation from the cycle of
  rebirth, giving the city a uniquely central place in Hindu death
  rites alongside its status as a living pilgrimage destination.
  Manikarnika Ghat, the city's primary cremation ground, and
  Dashashwamedh Ghat, the site of the nightly Ganga Aarti, are both
  central to this identity. Beyond Shiva worship, the city also gathers
  significant Shakti devotion (Annapurna, Vishalakshi — one of the 51
  Shakti Peethas) and Vaishnava and reformist traditions, giving
  Varanasi a genuinely multi-tradition religious character within one
  city, distinct from most single-deity pilgrimage towns.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable
  weather; Dev Deepawali (the "Diwali of the Gods," roughly 15 days
  after Diwali, when the ghats are lit with thousands of lamps) and
  Maha Shivratri are the city's most significant annual observances.

  **Getting there:** Varanasi has its own airport (Lal Bahadur Shastri
  International Airport) and is a major railway junction, well
  connected to Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai.

  **Getting around:** The old city's ghats and narrow galis are best
  explored on foot and by hired boat; the wider city is served by
  auto-rickshaws and taxis.
