overview: |
  Rishikesh, on the banks of the Ganges in Uttarakhand, is known both
  as the "Yoga Capital of the World" and as the gateway to the Char
  Dham of Uttarakhand pilgrimage circuit. Since 2015, Rishikesh and
  neighbouring Haridwar have jointly held the designation of India's
  "Twin National Heritage Cities."

history: |
  The name "Hrishikesh" (Rishikesh) is understood to derive from
  "Hrishik" (senses) and "Ish" (master), together meaning "Lord/Master
  of the Senses," a title of Vishnu. Local legend holds that Rama
  performed penance here to atone for killing Ravana during the events
  of the Ramayana. Bharat Mandir, dedicated to Vishnu, is traditionally
  credited to Adi Shankaracharya in the 12th century and is regarded as
  one of the town's oldest temples.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Rishikesh's spiritual character combines ancient temple tradition
  with a major modern yoga and meditation culture, drawing both Hindu
  pilgrims and international visitors. The Ganga Aarti at Parmarth
  Niketan ashram, held every evening with chanting, floating diyas, and
  participation from the ashram's school, is one of the town's most
  widely attended devotional events. As the last major town before the
  Himalayan approach to Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath,
  Rishikesh also functions as the practical and spiritual starting
  point for the Char Dham of Uttarakhand yatra.
