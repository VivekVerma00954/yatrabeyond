overview: |
  Puri, on the eastern coast of Odisha, is home to the Jagannath
  Temple, one of the four Char Dham of India alongside Badrinath,
  Dwarka, and Rameswaram. Known in earlier sources as Purushottama
  Kshetra or Nilachala, the site carried religious significance even
  before the present temple's construction, and Puri remains one of the
  most visited pilgrimage cities in eastern India, famous worldwide for
  its annual Rath Yatra chariot festival.

history: |
  The present Jagannath Temple was built in the 12th century CE by
  Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva. Its religious significance predates
  this construction: the Skanda Purana's Utkal Khanda devotes a section
  (the Purushottama Kshetra Mahatmya) to the site, and the Narada
  Purana and Matsya Purana similarly reference Purushottama Kshetra as
  sacred ground dedicated to Vishnu.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Jagannath Temple's inner sanctum houses three deities together:
  Jagannath, his sister Subhadra, and his elder brother Balabhadra,
  each carved from sacred neem-wood logs (daru) with a distinctive,
  simplified visual form. The temple's Kalinga-style architecture
  combines Rekha and Pidha Deula styles. The annual Rath Yatra, in
  which the three deities are drawn through the city in massive,
  ornately decorated chariots, is one of Hinduism's largest and most
  widely known festivals, drawing enormous crowds from across India and
  internationally.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to February offers the most comfortable
  coastal weather; Rath Yatra (June-July) is Puri's largest and most
  famous annual event by far, though it also brings by far the heaviest
  crowds.

  **Getting there:** Puri has its own railway station roughly 3 km from
  the Jagannath Temple, with direct trains from Kolkata, Patna, Delhi,
  Chennai, and Bhubaneswar, which has the nearest major airport.
