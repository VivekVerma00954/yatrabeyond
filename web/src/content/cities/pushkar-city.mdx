overview: |
  Pushkar, in Ajmer district, Rajasthan, is home to one of the only
  major temples in the world dedicated to Brahma, the creator god, and
  is built around Pushkar Lake, described in Hindu scripture as
  "Tirtha Raj," the king of all pilgrimage sites. Hindu tradition holds
  a pilgrimage to Pushkar to be among the most complete acts of
  devotion a person can undertake.

history: |
  According to the Padma Purana, Brahma descended to earth to perform a
  sacred yagna (fire ritual) and chose Pushkar after a lotus flower
  fell from his hand, forming the lake. When his consort Savitri was
  delayed for the ceremony, Brahma married a local woman, Gayatri, to
  complete the required rites; Savitri, in anger, cursed Brahma to
  never be worshipped anywhere else on Earth except Pushkar, which
  local tradition credits as the reason Pushkar's Brahma Temple remains
  so rare. The present temple structure dates in part to the 14th
  century, though the site's own sacred association is held to be far
  older.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Pushkar Lake, semi-circular and ringed by 52 bathing ghats and, by
  local count, over 400 temples, is the town's central sacred feature;
  a ritual bath in the lake is considered an essential part of a
  complete Pushkar pilgrimage. Beyond the Brahma Temple itself, the
  hilltop Gayatri and Savitri temples (Brahma's two consorts in this
  legend) sit on Nilgiri and Ratnagiri hills respectively, and the
  Varaha Temple, one of Pushkar's oldest, honours Vishnu's boar
  incarnation. Kartik Purnima is the town's most significant religious
  observance, drawing large numbers of pilgrims for ritual bathing, and
  coincides closely with the internationally known Pushkar Camel Fair.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable

  **Getting there:** Pushkar is reached by road from Ajmer, a short
  distance away, which has the nearest railway station; Jaipur has the
  nearest major airport.
