overview: |
  Haridwar, meaning "Gateway to the Lord" ("Hari" + "Dwar"), sits on the
  banks of the Ganges in Uttarakhand at the point where the river
  leaves the Himalayan foothills for the plains. One of Hinduism's
  seven sacred cities and a gateway to the wider Char Dham of
  Uttarakhand pilgrimage circuit, it is best known for Har Ki Pauri
  ghat and as one of the four rotating sites of the Kumbh Mela, the
  largest religious gathering on Earth.

history: |
  Haridwar's origins are difficult to date precisely, with some
  tradition placing habitation here as far back as pre-Vedic times, and
  other accounts crediting King Vikramaditya with establishing the city
  in its present form around the 1st century CE. Har Ki Pauri, the
  city's central ghat, is traditionally held to mark the exact spot
  where the Ganges leaves the mountains, and where Vishnu is said to
  have left a footprint in the riverbed.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Haridwar is one of the four sites (alongside Prayagraj, Ujjain, and
  Nashik) where, according to legend, drops of Amrita (the nectar of
  immortality) fell from the Kumbha (pitcher) carried by Garuda, giving
  rise to the Kumbh Mela cycle; the precise spot associated with this
  legend is Brahma Kund, within Har Ki Pauri. The city's hilltop Shakti
  temples, Mansa Devi and Chandi Devi, are both regarded as Siddh
  Peethas, believed to fulfil devotees' wishes, alongside Maya Devi
  Temple nearby. The evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri, with
  thousands of floating lamps set on the river, is one of Haridwar's
  most significant devotional and visitor experiences.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable
  weather; the Kumbh Mela (every 12 years) and Ardh Kumbh Mela (every 6
  years) are Haridwar's largest observances by a wide margin, and
  Ganga Dussehra is also significant.

  **Getting there:** Haridwar has its own railway station with strong
  connectivity, and is a common starting point for the Char Dham of
  Uttarakhand yatra; the nearest airport is Dehradun's Jolly Grant
  Airport.
