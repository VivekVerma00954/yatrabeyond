overview: |
  Ayodhya, on the banks of the Saryu River in Uttar Pradesh, is
  traditionally revered as the birthplace of Rama and the capital of
  his kingdom, making it one of the most significant Vaishnava
  pilgrimage cities in India. According to tradition, it was founded by
  the legendary king Manu and served as capital of the ancient Kosala
  kingdom; it has been a major pilgrimage destination for centuries and
  has seen dramatically increased visitor numbers since the January
  2024 consecration of the Ram Mandir at the Ram Janmabhoomi site.

history: |
  Ayodhya's history spans the rise and fall of several dynasties across
  the ancient and medieval periods, including the Mauryas and Guptas,
  and later Mughal-era rule. Its identity as Rama's birthplace has made
  the Ram Janmabhoomi site itself the subject of the modern era's most
  significant and long-running religious-legal dispute, resolved by
  the Supreme Court of India in 2019 in favour of temple construction,
  culminating in the Ram Mandir's January 2024 consecration.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Beyond the Ram Mandir itself, Ayodhya's wider religious landscape
  includes Hanuman Garhi (traditionally visited first, as Hanuman is
  held to be the city's eternal protector), Kanak Bhawan (a temple
  tradition holds was gifted to Sita by Queen Kaikeyi), Nageshwarnath
  Temple (a Shiva temple traditionally founded by Kush, Rama's son),
  and Treta Ke Thakur (housing ancient black-stone idols associated
  with the Treta Yuga). The evening aarti at Ram Ki Paidi on the Saryu,
  with floating diyas and devotional chanting, is a major devotional
  and cultural event in its own right.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable

  **Getting there:** Ayodhya has its own airport (Maharishi Valmiki
  International Airport) and railway station, with strong road and
  rail connectivity to Lucknow and the wider region.
