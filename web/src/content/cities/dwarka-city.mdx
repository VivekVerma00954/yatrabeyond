overview: |
  Dwarka, on the western coast of Gujarat, is revered as the legendary
  capital of Krishna's kingdom and is one of the four Char Dham of
  India, alongside Badrinath, Puri, and Rameswaram. Its name is
  popularly understood to mean "gateway to heaven" or "gateway to
  liberation." It is also counted among the seven Mokshada
  ("liberation-bestowing") cities of Hinduism, alongside Ayodhya,
  Mathura, Haridwar, Varanasi, Kanchipuram, and Ujjain.

history: |
  According to the Mahabharata tradition, Krishna established Dwarka as
  his capital around the age of 29 and ruled from the city for roughly
  60 years before the Kurukshetra war. The present Dwarkadhish Temple
  (Jagat Mandir) reflects Chalukya-style architecture, and Adi
  Shankaracharya is said to have visited the temple in the 8th century
  CE, formally designating it one of the four Char Dham.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Dwarkadhish Temple, the five-storey main shrine built in limestone
  and sand, is Dwarka's central pilgrimage site, dedicated to Krishna.
  Nageshwar Jyotirlinga, one of Shiva's twelve principal shrines, sits
  nearby and carries its own distinct origin legend. Rukmini Devi
  Temple, dedicated to Krishna's chief consort Rukmini a short distance
  from the main temple, is said to be the only temple in India
  dedicated specifically to her. Bhadkeshwar Mahadev Temple, a coastal
  Shiva shrine, is notable for being submerged by the sea during
  monsoon, understood by devotees as a natural abhishek (ritual
  bathing) of the deity.
