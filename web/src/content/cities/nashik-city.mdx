overview: |
  Nashik, in northwestern Maharashtra, is one of the four cities
  hosting the Kumbh Mela cycle (as the Nashik-Trimbakeshwar Simhastha),
  alongside Haridwar, Prayagraj, and Ujjain. Its religious identity is
  closely tied to the Ramayana, where the city's older name, "Nasik,"
  is said to derive from the episode in which Rama's brother Lakshmana
  severed the nose (nasika) of the demoness Shurpanakha while the
  brothers resided nearby at Panchavati during their exile.

history: |
  Trimbakeshwar Temple, near the source of the Godavari river at
  Brahmagiri mountain, roughly 30 km from central Nashik, was built by
  the third Peshwa, Balaji Bajirao, between 1740 and 1760, on the site
  of an earlier, older temple. As one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, it is
  among the most significant Shiva shrines in India.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Beyond Trimbakeshwar, Nashik's Panchavati area (the Ramayana-linked
  exile site) includes Kalaram Temple, dedicated to Rama, and is closely
  associated with Ramkund, a sacred Godavari bathing ghat used during
  the Kumbh Mela and linked in tradition to Rama's own rites. Nashik
  district is also home to Saptashrungi Temple, a major Shakti shrine
  roughly 60 km from the city. The Nashik-Trimbakeshwar Simhastha Kumbh
  Mela, held every 12 years, draws pilgrims for ritual bathing at both
  the Godavari in Nashik and at Trimbakeshwar itself.
