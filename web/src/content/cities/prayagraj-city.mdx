overview: |
  Prayagraj, formerly known as Allahabad (renamed in 2018, restoring
  its ancient name), sits at the Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the
  Ganga, Yamuna, and the traditionally invisible, underground
  Saraswati. Referred to in Hindu tradition as Tirtharaj, "King of
  Pilgrimages," the city is mentioned across the Rigveda, Mahabharata,
  Ramayana, and Puranas, and hosts the Kumbh Mela, the largest
  pilgrimage gathering on Earth.

history: |
  The city's older name, Prayag, means "land of sacrifices"; Hindu
  scripture holds that Brahma performed a yajna here before the
  creation of the universe, giving the site its foundational sanctity.
  Akbar's Fort, a significant Mughal-era structure near the Sangam,
  reflects the city's later historical layers, alongside Anand Bhavan,
  the historic Nehru family residence associated with India's freedom
  struggle.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Bathing at the Triveni Sangam is believed to destroy sins and confer
  liberation, making it the devotional and ritual centre of the city.
  Prayagraj hosts the Maha Kumbh Mela every 12 years and the Ardh Kumbh
  every 6 years, drawing over 100 million pilgrims across the festival
  period, the largest religious gathering recorded anywhere in the
  world; the annual Magh Mela, held every January-February, is a
  smaller but still major recurring observance at the same site. The
  city's Shakti tradition is represented by Alopi Devi and Lalita Devi
  temples, both linked (via differing accounts) to the Sati Shakti
  Peetha legend.

pilgrimageInfo: |
  **When to visit:** October to March offers the most comfortable

  **Getting there:** Prayagraj has its own airport and is a major
  railway junction, well connected to Delhi, Varanasi, and Kolkata.
