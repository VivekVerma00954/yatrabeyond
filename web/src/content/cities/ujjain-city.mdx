overview: |
  Ujjain, on the banks of the Kshipra river in Madhya Pradesh, was
  known in ancient times as Avantika and served as a major political,
  cultural, and astronomical centre under the Mauryan and Gupta
  empires. It is one of the Sapta Puri, the seven sacred cities of
  Hinduism believed to grant salvation, and is home to the
  Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, one of Shiva's twelve most significant
  shrines.

history: |
  Ujjain's long history as a centre of learning and astronomy runs
  alongside its religious significance; the city has hosted major
  regional powers across the ancient and medieval periods and remains
  one of the four rotating sites of the Kumbh Mela cycle (held here as
  the Simhastha Kumbh Mela), alongside Haridwar, Prayagraj, and Nashik.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Mahakaleshwar Temple, Ujjain's central Shiva shrine, is known
  particularly for its Bhasma Aarti, an early-morning ritual in which
  the deity is worshipped with sacred ash. The city's Kal Bhairav
  Temple, dedicated to a fierce guardian form of Shiva, is well known
  for its distinctive and long-standing tradition of offering liquor to
  the deity; this is a separate and unrelated temple from the
  similarly named Kaal Bhairav Temple in Varanasi. Ram Ghat, on the
  Kshipra, is the city's principal river ghat and its main site during
  the Simhastha Kumbh Mela.
