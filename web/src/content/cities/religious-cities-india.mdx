history: |
  Seven of India's cities are traditionally grouped as the Sapta Puri
  (seven sacred cities), believed to grant moksha to those who die
  within their boundaries: Ayodhya, Mathura, Haridwar, Varanasi (Kashi),
  Kanchipuram, Ujjain (Avantika), and Dwarka. This platform's initial
  City batch covers five of these seven directly (Ayodhya, Mathura,
  Haridwar, Varanasi, Ujjain, and Dwarka — six, in fact), plus several
  further major pilgrimage cities identified for their existing depth
  of temple coverage on this platform: Vrindavan, Rishikesh, Pushkar,
  Prayagraj, Nashik, and Gaya. Kanchipuram remains a future addition.

spiritualSignificance: |
  Four of these cities (Haridwar, Prayagraj, Ujjain, and Nashik) rotate
  as hosts of the Kumbh Mela, the largest recurring religious gathering
  on Earth. Four more (Badrinath, Dwarka, Puri, and Rameswaram) form
  the Char Dham of India, already covered on this platform as an
  overview; of these, Dwarka and Puri are additionally covered here as
  full City pages given their standing as cities in their own right,
  not only temple sites.
